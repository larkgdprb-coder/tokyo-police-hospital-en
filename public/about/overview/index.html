Historical Timeline

YearEvent
1920Planning began for a dedicated hospital for the Tokyo Metropolitan Police Department
1929Tokyo Police Hospital officially founded (March 18) in Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo
1945Hospital survived World War II; continues operations
1960s–70sExpansion of clinical departments and nursing staff
1980sEstablishment of specialized centers; adoption of new diagnostic technologies
1990sIntroduction of advanced imaging equipment (MRI, CT); expanded emergency services
2000Began transition planning for new facility
2008Relocation to current site in Nakano-ku; new 9-story building opened
2010Received accreditation from the Japan Council for Quality Health Care (JCQHC)
2015Introduction of da Vinci robotic surgical system
2018Designated as Cancer Designated Hospital
2020Establishment of COVID-19 response protocols; continues to serve community through pandemic
2023Continued quality improvement; updated DPC classification; expansion of robotic surgery program

Hospital Designation & Certifications

Tokyo Police Hospital holds numerous official designations:

  • Acute Emergency Hospital (Level 2 Emergency)
  • DPC Hospital (Standard DPC Group)
  • Cancer Designated Hospital
  • Stroke Center
  • Disaster Medical Assistance Team (DMAT) Base Hospital
  • JCQHC-Accredited Hospital
  • Referral-Focused Medical Institution
