What Is a Referral-Focused Medical Institution?

Tokyo Police Hospital has been designated as a Referral-Focused Medical Institution (Shōkai Jushū Jūten Iryō Kikan, 紹介受診重点医療機関) under Japan’s national healthcare framework.

This designation recognizes hospitals that primarily provide advanced and specialized medical care for complex cases requiring hospital-level expertise, while encouraging patients with stable or routine conditions to consult with local clinics and general practitioners.

Why This Designation Matters

Japan’s healthcare system is structured so that:

  • Local clinics and primary care physicians handle everyday health concerns, preventive care, and the management of stable chronic conditions.
  • Designated hospitals like Tokyo Police Hospital focus on complex diagnoses, surgical procedures, acute care, and specialist consultations.

By following this structure, patients receive the most appropriate level of care, wait times are reduced, and hospital resources are available for those who need them most.

What This Means for Patients

If you currently visit Tokyo Police Hospital regularly for a stable chronic condition (e.g., hypertension, diabetes, hyperlipidemia) that does not require specialist-level care:

  • We may recommend that you transition your ongoing care to a local clinic.
  • Our hospital will provide a return referral letter and full medical records to your chosen local physician.
  • You can return to Tokyo Police Hospital at any time if your condition changes or specialist care becomes necessary.

Surcharges for Visits Without Referral

As part of this policy, patients visiting without a referral letter are subject to:

Surcharge TypeAmount
Initial consultation surcharge¥7,700 (tax included)
Re-examination surcharge (certain cases)¥3,300 (tax included)

These surcharges are waived for patients arriving with a referral letter, emergency cases, and other specific circumstances.

Our Commitment to Patients

Even with this designation, we remain fully committed to providing the highest quality of care to every patient. If you have any concerns about your care or the referral process, please speak with your attending physician or contact our Medical Liaison Office at 03-5343-5760.

We appreciate your understanding and cooperation in helping us maintain efficient and effective healthcare for all.
