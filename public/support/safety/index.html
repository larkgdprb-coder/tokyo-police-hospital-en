Patient Safety at Tokyo Police Hospital

Patient safety is the highest priority at Tokyo Police Hospital. We maintain a comprehensive, hospital-wide safety management program to prevent errors, identify risks, and continuously improve the quality of care we deliver.

Our Safety Management System

Safety Management Committee

The Patient Safety Management Committee oversees all aspects of safety across the hospital. This committee includes the hospital director, department heads, nursing leadership, pharmacists, and other key staff.

Responsibilities include:

  • Establishing and reviewing safety policies and procedures
  • Analyzing incident reports and implementing corrective actions
  • Overseeing risk management programs
  • Reporting safety performance to hospital leadership

Incident Reporting System

All staff are encouraged and expected to report any incidents, near-misses, or unsafe conditions through our voluntary, non-punitive incident reporting system. Reports are reviewed and used to identify systemic issues and implement improvements.

Analysis of incidents is conducted using methodologies including Root Cause Analysis (RCA) for serious events.

Safety Education & Training

All staff complete mandatory patient safety training, including:

  • Medication safety
  • Fall prevention
  • Infection control
  • Communication and handover protocols (SBAR)
  • Emergency response procedures

How You Can Help Keep Care Safe

Patients and families play a vital role in patient safety. We encourage you to:

  1. Tell us who you are — Always verify that staff confirm your name and date of birth before any procedure, medication, or test.
  2. Speak up — If something doesn’t seem right, say so. Never hesitate to ask questions.
  3. Know your medications — Understand what medications you are taking and why.
  4. Prevent falls — Call for a nurse rather than getting up alone if you are unsteady. Use the call button.
  5. Keep hands clean — Use hand sanitizer before eating and after using the bathroom.
  6. Tell us about allergies — Inform all staff about any known drug, food, or material allergies.

Fall Prevention

Falls are one of the most common types of hospital incidents. We take the following measures to reduce fall risk:

  • Assessment of every inpatient’s fall risk on admission
  • Non-slip footwear for all inpatients
  • Call buttons within reach at all times
  • Bed rails adjusted to patient needs
  • Adequate lighting in rooms and corridors
  • Regular safety checks by nursing staff

Please do not hesitate to call for assistance before getting out of bed.

Medication Safety

Our pharmacy team works closely with physicians and nurses to:

  • Verify all prescriptions before dispensing
  • Check for drug interactions and allergies
  • Counsel patients on correct medication use
  • Prevent medication errors through barcode verification systems

Patient Safety Reporting & Feedback

If you experience or observe a safety concern during your visit or stay, please report it immediately to any staff member. You may also contact the Patient Relations Desk or submit a written concern via our suggestion box.

Your feedback helps us improve safety for everyone.
