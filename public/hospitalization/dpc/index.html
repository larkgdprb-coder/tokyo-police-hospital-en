What Is the DPC Payment System?

Tokyo Police Hospital uses the Diagnosis Procedure Combination / Per-Diem Payment System (DPC/PDPS) to calculate inpatient medical fees. This system is applied at designated hospitals throughout Japan.

How DPC Works

Under the DPC system, your daily hospitalization cost is calculated using a standardized rate based on:

  1. Your primary diagnosis (classified using the DPC code system)
  2. Your treatment procedures (surgery, specific therapies, etc.)
  3. The length of your stay

Rather than billing individually for each examination, injection, or procedure, DPC consolidates most routine inpatient costs into a single daily package rate. This makes the total cost more predictable and often more affordable than fee-for-service billing.

What Is Included in the DPC Rate

The daily DPC rate typically includes:

  • Room and board (standard 4-bed room)
  • Basic nursing care
  • Routine diagnostic tests (blood tests, X-rays, etc.)
  • Routine medications administered during hospitalization
  • Basic medical management

What Is NOT Included (Billed Separately)

Some items are billed separately under the standard fee-for-service system, including:

  • Surgery fees
  • Anesthesia fees
  • High-cost specialty treatments
  • Specific advanced diagnostic procedures
  • Private room fees (see Room Types)
  • Dental treatment
  • Patient convenience items (TV rental, etc.)

DPC and Your Out-of-Pocket Cost

Under Japan’s public health insurance system, you pay a co-payment (typically 10%, 20%, or 30% depending on your insurance and age). The DPC rate is used to calculate the total cost, from which your co-payment is derived.

If your total medical costs exceed the High-Cost Medical Expense threshold (Kōgaku Ryōyōhi) for the month, you may be eligible for a rebate or reduction. Please consult the billing section or a medical social worker for assistance.

Hospital Classification

Tokyo Police Hospital is classified under the Standard DPC Group, reflecting our hospital’s medical function level and quality benchmarks.

Our Medical Institution Coefficient is 1.5771, which is one of the factors used to calculate the DPC rate applicable to our hospital.

Questions About Your Bill

If you have questions about your hospital bill or the DPC system, please contact:

  • Billing & Accounting Counter (1st Floor)
  • Phone: 03-5343-5611 (main) → ask for accounting
  • Hours: Monday – Saturday, 8:00 – 17:00

An itemized bill is available free of charge upon request. Please ask the accounting staff if you would like a detailed breakdown of your charges.
