## About obstetrics and gynecology

Obstetrics and Gynecology is the department in charge of women's lifelong medical care that supports women throughout their lives and the birth of a new life. We not only deal with the illness or childbirth that is in front of us, but we also want to help each woman from a holistic perspective, hoping that each woman can live a healthier and more fulfilling life, both physically and mentally. In addition, as a core hospital that is close to the local community and workplace, we work closely with surrounding medical institutions and strive to be a place where everyone can visit with peace of mind. Our department is a affiliated hospital of the Department of Obstetrics and Gynecology of the University of Tokyo School of Medicine, and provides team medical care centered on doctors dispatched from the university's medical bureau. Female and male doctors work together by leveraging their respective strengths and specialties, and maintain good cooperation with other doctors and co-medical staff, aiming to provide well-balanced, high-quality medical care.

## Main diseases examined in obstetrics and gynecology

Normal pregnancy, abnormal pregnancy (miscarriage, ectopic pregnancy, threatened preterm labor, etc.), normal/abnormal delivery, complications during pregnancy and puerperium (morning sickness, gestational diabetes, gestational hypertension syndrome, pregnancy complicated by other medical conditions, postpartum depression, mastitis, etc.). Irregular menstruation, dysmenorrhea, premenstrual syndrome, infertility, uterine fibroids, adenomyosis, ovarian cysts, endometriosis, cervical polyps, endometrial polyps, cervical dysplasia, endometrial hyperplasia, cervical cancer, endometrial cancer, ovarian cancer, fallopian tube cancer, peritoneal cancer, vaginal cancer, vulvitis, vaginitis, vulva Thorin's gland abscess, endometritis/pyometritis, adnexitis/salpingitis, pelvic peritonitis, menopausal symptoms, osteoporosis, uterine prolapse, cystocele/rectal aneurysm, gynecological psychosomatic disorders, sexual violence victim/DV victim consultation, cervical cancer prevention vaccine, rubella prevention vaccine, influenza prevention vaccination, etc.

## Treatment contents and characteristics

### About obstetrics

Our hospital's obstetrics department is based on safe and secure childbirth, but it also features a homely and detailed management and care during pregnancy, childbirth, and postpartum period, which is a little different from large-scale perinatal centers.

During prenatal checkups, we strive to minimize the burden on pregnant women by using just the right number of test items and a unified management policy, and our midwives provide cheerful and courteous care every time. Our friendly midwife staff has a reputation for seamless support from prenatal to postnatal, as well as dedicated breastfeeding guidance and breast care. In addition, we work with specialists in other departments within the hospital to ensure that pregnant women with complications such as diabetes, thyroid disease, mental illness, etc. can give birth at our hospital as much as possible. We also provide prenatal checkups and breast care for those who are planning to return home to give birth in another prefecture or who have given birth at another hospital.

In order to have a normal birth as safely as possible, our team holds regular conferences to devise delivery timing and delivery methods, and we are constantly striving to maintain and improve techniques for rapid delivery such as vacuum delivery and forceps delivery. As a result, we choose caesarean section delivery after careful consideration of medical necessity, and at the same time, when necessary, we make decisions and make proposals as early as possible, and we strive to implement the procedure at a safe time. Our experienced obstetricians and gynecologists, with the cooperation of our hospital anesthesiologists, are beginning to work on painless childbirth, focusing on cases where it is considered safer to relieve labor pains due to maternal complications and other circumstances. If you would like to have a painless delivery, please consult us during your prenatal checkup. Obstetricians and gynecologists are always on-site at the hospital 24 hours a day, 365 days a year, and we also have on-call doctors waiting at home to rush in if necessary. All newborns are carefully monitored and managed by pediatricians from a professional standpoint. In particular, regarding respiratory management, not only pediatricians and obstetricians and gynecologists, but also all midwives, regularly undergo academically certified neonatal resuscitation (NCPR) training.We also have midwives who are NCPR instructors, and at the same time, we frequently hold in-hospital study sessions on respiratory management, etc. led by pediatricians, in an effort to improve safety.

In addition, all delivery rooms are LDR, which allows you to spend the entire time with your family in the same private room from the start of labor to the end of delivery.

Although our hospital cannot handle premature births of less than 10 months that require NICU (neonatal intensive care unit) or newborns requiring respiratory care, please rest assured that in such cases, we utilize Tokyo's perinatal network to quickly and appropriately transport the mother and newborn to a local perinatal center.

On the other hand, for abnormal pregnancies, we perform surgery for ectopic pregnancies (laparoscopically or through laparotomy), miscarriage surgery, and surgery and treatment for trophoblastic diseases such as hydatidiform mole. All surgeries are performed under anesthesia by an anesthesiologist, and for early miscarriages, we also use manual vacuum aspiration (MVA), which causes less damage to the uterus. In the case of intrauterine fetal death after the second trimester (12 weeks), delivery will be performed using uterotonic drugs (vaginal suppositories, etc.). In addition, if you wish to undergo miscarriage surgery, you can undergo chromosome testing of villi and fetal tissue, and we also provide consultation regarding subsequent pregnancies. *If you have a habitual miscarriage, testing at our hospital may be covered by the Tokyo Metropolitan Infertility Testing Subsidy Project, so please consult your doctor when visiting the outpatient clinic (we do not accept phone inquiries).

There are two methods for induced abortion in the first trimester: surgery (MVA) and the use of oral abortion pills (up to 9 weeks and 0 days). Our hospital has certain restrictions regarding schedules, and both methods require hospitalization. <Estimated hospitalization and treatment costs> Surgery: Approximately 230,000 yen + tax ~ Oral abortion pill: Approximately 160,000 yen + tax ~. In addition, for induced abortions (delivery using vaginal suppositories) during the second trimester of pregnancy (12 weeks to 21 weeks), we mainly respond to people currently attending our hospital, referrals from affiliated medical institutions, people with medical circumstances, and those affiliated with the Tokyo Metropolitan Police Department. <Estimated hospitalization/treatment costs> Approximately 50,000 yen or more using the birth and childcare lump sum allowance. For more information, please visit our obstetrics and gynecology outpatient clinic and consult with us.

### About gynecology

Our hospital's gynecology department is conscious of its position as a core hospital in the region, and handles a wide range of medical treatment, from benign to malignant diseases, including emergency cases.

If you wish to continue conservative treatment (medication, etc.) as an outpatient, you can choose your doctor by appointment.

All gynecological examinations in our hospital's complete medical checkup are carried out by our full-time doctors, and if detailed examinations or treatment are required, we will continue to see you as an outpatient. We also accept secondary examinations (detailed examinations) for gynecological examinations at other hospitals, and we devise the minimum necessary and reliable treatment for cervical cell abnormalities (cervical dysplasia, carcinoma in situ), taking future pregnancies into consideration.

Regarding surgeries for benign diseases, we are actively incorporating laparoscopic surgery and hysteroscopic surgery, which require only small incisions and a short hospital stay, and in particular, we are working to increase the number of laparoscopic hysterectomies by selecting appropriate cases. In March 2022, we began robot-assisted surgery (da Vinci surgery) for total hysterectomies for benign uterine diseases. Robot-assisted surgery allows the surgeon to operate the forceps more freely and accurately than conventional laparoscopic surgery, allowing for less invasive and more advanced surgery. As there are unique complications and limitations, we carefully select cases with due consideration to safety. We also perform transvaginal surgery for uterine prolapse, cystocele, rectocele, etc. On the other hand, depending on the case, we may recommend conventional laparotomy or small laparotomy, with safe and reliable treatment as our top priority.

From April 2022, many infertility treatments will be covered by insurance. At our hospital, we are in charge of initial infertility tests and general infertility treatment, and at the same time, for patients with gynecological diseases who wish to have children, we propose surgeries and drug treatments with an eye toward future pregnancy, and as necessary, we collaborate with facilities that perform advanced infertility treatment.

For malignant diseases, in addition to surgery, we can also provide radiation therapy by a radiologist and chemotherapy (anticancer drug treatment) in the outpatient department or in the ward. Our palliative care team will work with you to treat your pain and suffering.

A feature of our hospital is that we can quickly and easily obtain support from other specialists, including internal medicine, surgery, and urology, as needed, whether it is for surgery or inpatient treatment.We strive to provide higher quality medical care through a multidisciplinary team.
