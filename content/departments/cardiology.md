## Treatment contents and characteristics

In the Department of Cardiology, we mainly treat ischemic heart diseases such as myocardial infarction and angina pectoris, heart diseases such as arrhythmia, heart failure, valvular heart disease, and cardiomyopathy, and peripheral vascular diseases such as arteriosclerosis obliterans. Cardiovascular diseases are characterized by sudden onset and sudden changes in the condition, and there are many cases where differences in initial treatment and response to changes in the condition can be the difference between life and death. In particular, in cases of acute myocardial infarction, unstable angina, acute heart failure, severe arrhythmia, pulmonary thromboembolism, etc., we have multiple cardiovascular staff and medical doctors to ensure prompt diagnosis and thorough acute-phase treatment, and we actively accept emergency cases.

### About ischemic heart disease

In recent years, with the increase in "lifestyle-related diseases", ischemic heart disease is increasing and the number of patients is getting younger. In our department, we provide a wide range of treatments to prevent the onset of the disease, including controlling coronary risk factors (hypertension, diabetes, dyslipidemia), smoking cessation guidance, and nutritional guidance. On the other hand, for cases of acute myocardial infarction or unstable angina, emergency cardiac catheterization and coronary artery intervention are performed by specialists day and night. In addition, for ordinary ischemic heart disease (stable angina pectoris, old myocardial infarction), we comprehensively evaluate the results of exercise electrocardiograms, myocardial scintigrams, cardiac CT, cardiac catheterization, etc., and perform minimally invasive, safe, and reliable coronary artery interventions.

### About arrhythmia

We have specialized staff for the diagnosis and treatment of arrhythmia, and we perform diagnosis using Holter electrocardiograms and electrophysiological tests, pacemaker treatment for bradyarrhythmia, and catheter ablation, which is a radical treatment for tachyarrhythmia. We are focusing on drug therapy for various other arrhythmias, especially for atrial fibrillation, which is increasing as society ages, and we approach it from every angle. In the outpatient department, we have a pacemaker/ICD outpatient clinic (Friday afternoons) as a specialized outpatient clinic.

### About heart failure

As society ages, the number of elderly patients with heart failure is increasing. In our department, we work closely with nearby hospitals and clinics to proactively provide acute phase treatment for patients with acute heart failure and acute exacerbation of chronic heart failure.We also focus on outpatient treatment for patients with chronic heart failure, with the aim of preventing repeated heart failure hospitalizations. Furthermore, we have started hospitalization for tolvaptan, which is a procedure that is performed before the patient becomes emergency hospitalized due to heart failure.

### others

We actively diagnose and treat lower extremity arteriosclerosis obliterans (medication therapy and catheter treatment), which causes pain in the lower extremities when walking, as well as diagnose and treat lower extremity venous thrombosis, pulmonary embolism, and pulmonary hypertension.

We will continue to work day and night to maintain cooperation with local doctors and aim for community-based medical care that is closely connected to the local community, as well as our hospital's philosophy of ``improving the quality of medical care and patient satisfaction.''
