## About the emergency and intensive care department

Our hospital's emergency and intensive care department has an ER system under the supervision of an emergency department specialist. ER is an abbreviation for emergency room, and originally meant a medical treatment space, but the term has come to be used to describe the North American emergency treatment system as ER or ER-type treatment. The ER system means that a dedicated physician (ER physician) provides efficient medical treatment to all emergency patients. What makes our hospital different from the normal North American ER system is that if the affiliated emergency physician can take care of the patient in his/her specialized field, the patient will be directly taken care of in the emergency/intensive care department. In addition, the Emergency and Intensive Care Department is in charge of the Intensive Care Center (ICU/CCU), and is referred to in English as Emergency and Critical Medicine. Critically ill patients are treated in intensive care centers (ICU/CCU), and sometimes emergency surgery is also performed. Our emergency and intensive care physicians aim to become intensivists who handle critically ill patients and acute care surgeons who handle emergency surgeries, based on the foundation of ER physicians.

## Main diseases treated in emergency and intensive care departments

All regular emergency patients who come to the hospital by ambulance are eligible. The most common medical illnesses include dizziness, temporary loss of consciousness, gastrointestinal bleeding, dehydration, pneumonia, and respiratory failure, while the most common surgical illnesses include trauma, including simple dislocations and fractures, extremity injuries, and head, chest, and abdominal contusions. We also treat acute abdominal conditions such as acute pancreatitis and peritonitis, acute drug poisoning, rhabdomyolysis, skin and soft tissue infections, and environmental abnormalities such as heatstroke and hypothermia. Normally, the emergency department is solely in charge of emergency wards and general wards, but in severe cases, patients are treated in the intensive care center (ICU/CCU), making full use of advanced medical care such as artificial respiration management, various blood purification methods, percutaneous cardiopulmonary support (PCPS), and cerebral hypothermia therapy, in cooperation with specialists in each department.
