## Introduction

We have been involved in pediatric medical care in this region since 2008, and have been working with local medical institutions to provide medical care to protect the health of children. Full-time doctors are in charge of general outpatient and inpatient care, and part-time doctors are in charge of specialized outpatient care, and we strive every day to help all patients who visit our hospital.

## Everyone who visits pediatrics

We examine each patient as carefully as possible, and strive to respond appropriately to the various problems and illnesses that children are experiencing.

### Diseases that can be examined (◎ means there are specialized outpatient clinics)

〇 Infectious diseases 〇 Kawasaki disease 〇 Respiratory diseases (pneumonia, bronchial asthma, prolonged cough, etc.) 〇 Skin diseases (eczema, impetigo, hives, drug eruption, etc.) 〇 Digestive diseases (abdominal pain, diarrhea, constipation, irritable bowel syndrome, etc.) 〇Allergic diseases (food allergies, atopic dermatitis, allergic rhinitis, etc.) 〇Emergency diseases (dehydration, anaphylaxis, febrile convulsions, heat stroke, etc.) ◎ Kidney and urinary diseases (bedwetting, hematuria, proteinuria, etc.) ◎ Cardiovascular diseases (close examination for heart murmur, arrhythmia, congenital heart disease) ◎ Neurological diseases, developmental problems (convulsions, autism, ADHD, etc.) ◎ Mental diseases (anorexia nervosa, school refusal, etc.) 〇Others (headaches, night crying, tantrums, insomnia, lightheadedness, difficulty getting up, fatigue easily, etc.)

## To all medical institutions

### Information on specialized outpatient clinics

We ask that you refer patients to both the general and specialized outpatient clinics.

### Hospitalization information

The diseases for which hospitalization is possible are as follows.

Pneumonia, bronchitis, bronchial asthma, urinary tract infections, skin infections, enteritis, dehydration, acetonemic emesis, IgA vasculitis, nephrotic syndrome, Kawasaki disease, anorexia nervosa, etc.
