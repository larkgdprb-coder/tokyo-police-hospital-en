## About otorhinolaryngology

Otorhinolaryngology is a medical department that deals with a wide range of anatomical areas, including the ear, nose, mouth, throat, and neck. We treat various disorders related to sensory organ functions such as hearing, balance, smell, and taste, upper gastrointestinal functions such as mastication and swallowing, and upper respiratory functions such as spoken language and respiratory functions. In addition to general imaging tests (CT, MRI, ultrasound) and blood tests that are used in other medical departments, we perform diagnosis and treatment using a combination of tests specific to otolaryngology, such as hearing tests, balance function tests, and taste and smell tests.

## Treatment contents and characteristics

### 1. Endoscopic ear surgery

Endoscopic ear surgery is a new treatment method developed around 2010 that uses only an endoscope through the ear canal. An endoscope and instruments less than 3 mm in diameter are inserted through the ear canal, called the external auditory canal, and the surgery is performed while viewing images displayed on a monitor.

There are basically two types of ear surgery. Microscopic surgery and endoscopic surgery. Both have the same goal of "improving hearing," but their approaches are different. Microsurgery is performed by making an incision behind the ear and scraping the bone. In endoscopic surgery, the endoscope is inserted through the ear canal, so there is no need to make a large incision.

Diseases that can be treated with endoscopic ear surgery include chronic otitis media (inflammation of the middle ear that causes a hole in the eardrum), otosclerosis (hardening of the ear tissue), ossicular dissection (a small bone that transmits sound is misaligned), and middle ear cholesteatoma (a pearl-like lump that forms near the eardrum).

The advantages of endoscopic surgery over conventional microscopic surgery include not drilling into the bone from outside the body, less stress on the body, less pain, shorter hospital stay, and faster recovery.

For details, please refer to [here] (external site).

Depending on the condition, it may be difficult to perform surgery using an endoscope alone, but in that case, the surgery may be performed in combination with conventional microsurgery.

### 3. Endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery

At our hospital, we perform nasal and sinus surgery under general anesthesia using a surgical endoscope with 4K image quality. At our hospital, we basically provide inpatient treatment in consideration of the risks of general anesthesia and the safety of dealing with postoperative bleeding. Surgery days are Wednesdays and Fridays, and the hospital stay is usually 4 to 8 days. (The day before the surgery, the day of the surgery, and you will be discharged after several days of follow-up observation.) Depending on the amount of blood lost during the surgery, during your hospitalization, we will put a removable sponge or naturally dissolving cotton into your nose and apply pressure from the inside. During this time, you will be breathing through your mouth. You can take a shower from the day after the surgery. After a few days of compression, remove the sponge. If there is no bleeding after this, you will be discharged from the hospital the next day. Even after being discharged from the hospital, a small amount of bleeding will continue for a certain period of time. Also, nasal congestion may become worse temporarily. It takes approximately two months for the mucous membranes of the sinuses to stabilize. Until then, the nasal mucosa is prone to inflammation and adhesions, so please gargle your nose at home.

### 4. Treatment for refractory allergic rhinitis

#### ●Carbon dioxide laser

Nasal congestion is one of the symptoms of allergic rhinitis that is difficult to respond to medications. For those who are suffering from intractable nasal congestion, we offer same-day laser treatment for the inferior turbinate mucosa. We also treat rhinitis for people who have severe hay fever and for whom the only effective treatment is medication that makes them sleepy every year, or for people who are thinking of getting pregnant and want to avoid using medications as much as possible. (Please consult with us as there may be times when it is difficult to perform the procedure depending on your allergies.)

#### ●Sublingual immunotherapy

In recent years, "sublingual immunotherapy" has emerged as a new treatment for allergic rhinitis. This is a type of desensitization therapy, in which the substance that causes the allergy (allergen) is administered in small doses. This treatment allows your body to get used to the allergen and relieves allergy symptoms. If treated correctly for several years, it can be expected to be effective in curing allergic symptoms or suppressing them for a long period of time.

#### ●Xolair® (generic name: omalizumab) injection

"Xolair" is an injectable drug that can be used to control symptoms of severe seasonal allergic rhinitis. Unlike oral medications or nasal sprays, it suppresses the source of allergic reactions that cause inflammation. Please note that there are restrictions on the conditions for use, so if the conditions are met, you can use the product by conducting an allergy test using a blood sample, and by asking about the degree of allergic rhinitis symptoms, treatment history, and weight.

For details, please refer to [here] (external site).

#### ●Posterior nasal nerve transection

The posterior nasal nerve is the nerve that controls runny nose and sneezing. Posterior nasal nerve transection is a surgery that relieves nasal symptoms by cutting this nerve. It is performed in severe cases of nasal discharge and sneezing associated with allergic rhinitis. The surgery is performed under general anesthesia, so hospitalization is required.

### 5. Treatment for refractory chronic sinusitis

Dupixent® (generic name: dupilumab) or Nucala® (generic name: mepolizumab) is a new treatment for eosinophilic sinusitis. It is a subcutaneous injection given every 2 to 4 weeks. Used for patients with moderate or more severe eosinophilic sinusitis, especially for cases of recurrence after surgery. For more information on Dupixent®, please refer to [here] (external site).
