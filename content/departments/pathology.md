## Overview

The Pathology Department performs laboratory-based diagnosis through microscopic examination of tissue specimens, cytological samples, and body fluids. Our board-certified pathologists support all clinical departments by providing accurate diagnoses essential for patient management.

## Services

- Surgical pathology (examination of biopsy and surgical specimens)
- Cytopathology (Pap smear, fine needle aspiration cytology)
- Intraoperative frozen section diagnosis
- Autopsy pathology
- Immunohistochemistry and molecular pathology

## Clinical Role

Pathology is the foundation of cancer diagnosis and many other conditions. Our pathologists:
- Confirm diagnoses of cancer and other diseases from tissue samples
- Provide real-time frozen section analysis during surgery to guide surgical decisions
- Perform molecular testing to guide targeted cancer therapy
- Collaborate with clinical teams at multidisciplinary tumor board meetings

## Reporting

Pathology reports are sent directly to the requesting physician. Results are typically available within **3–5 business days** for routine cases. Urgent or frozen section results are communicated immediately.
