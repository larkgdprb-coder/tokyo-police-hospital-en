## About the Department of Pathology

- It was established in April 2008, when the current hospital was relocated and opened.
- We have two full-time pathologists (plus a part-time pathologist) and six cytotechnologists (who also serve as clinical laboratory technicians).
- Our job is to perform pathological diagnoses, or ``various medical judgments using microscopic pathology,'' in response to requests from each medical department.
- Our operating philosophy is user-oriented pathological diagnosis.User friendly pathology.

## Diseases examined in the pathology department

All of the patient's tissues and cells collected by each department for pathological diagnosis, as well as organs removed during surgery, are subject to pathological diagnosis. Therefore, at our hospital, we treat all diseases that require pathological diagnosis.

## Treatment contents and characteristics

### 1. The Department of Pathology is a new flagship medical department.

The advertised medical department is the name of the medical department that can be displayed by medical institutions. Since April 2008, the Medical Law and Ministry of Health, Labor and Welfare Ordinance has made it possible to become a designated department, and from the time our hospital opened, the former "Department of Pathology" was reorganized to the Department of Pathology. Although we do not have an outpatient department or ward, we perform pathological diagnosis work and report to the requesting department at the request of the department.

### 2. Pathological diagnosis is a diagnosis that applies pathology.

Diagnosis involves observing materials taken from the human body under a microscope using knowledge of pathology. In ancient medicine, pathology was a field of medical research that discussed the ``problems of disease,'' but in modern times, it has become a specialized field for understanding diseases based on the morphology (shape) of tissues and cells observed with the naked eye and under a microscope. On the other hand, diagnosis is not just naming a disease, but also includes all possible medical judgments such as the scope and severity of the lesion, estimation of the cause, outlook, the presence or absence of substances related to treatment, and the effectiveness of treatment.

### 3. Pathological diagnosis is classified into several types depending on the target of diagnosis.

Histological diagnosis is a diagnosis that uses tissue specimens prepared from small resected tissues (biopsies) or large resected organs. Cytology (cytology) is a diagnosis based on the observation of cells in urine or sputum, or cells collected by scraping a lesion or aspirating it with a needle. Pathological autopsy (autopsy) is the examination of the whole body of a deceased patient to diagnose the cause of the disease, pathological condition, and the overall picture of the course of the disease. The time required for reporting varies depending on the type of diagnosis.

### 4. All staff members are experts in pathological diagnosis.

We have two full-time pathologists and cytology specialists, a part-time pathologist, and five full-time cytotechnologists and clinical laboratory technicians. Pathologists are certified by the Japanese Society of Pathology, and cytodiagnostic specialists and cytotechnologists are certified by the Japanese Society of Clinical Cytology. In addition, the director is a pathologist training instructor.

### 5. Our operating philosophy is user-oriented pathology diagnosis.

- Pathological diagnosis that best corresponds to the requested content
- Standardized and rapid reporting
- Good communication with the referring doctor
- Cooperation system with outside pathologists
- Training to improve the quality of diagnosis
- Emphasis on pathological diagnosis education for trainee doctors
- Establishment of a system to train pathological diagnostic specialists from hospitals

### 6. There are many benefits of having a pathology department.

- Rapid intraoperative diagnosis is possible by applying freezing technology during surgery to diagnose resection margins in about 15 minutes.
- Diagnosis is highly reproducible and consistent as the same pathologist/cytotechnologist makes the diagnosis.
- Enables face-to-face communication between client physician and pathologist
- Easily obtain the clinical information you need
- Diagnosis that takes into account macroscopic findings is possible
