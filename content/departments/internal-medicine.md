## About general medicine internal medicine

### Medical care that examines people with reliable expertise

Tokyo Police Hospital General Medicine Department is a medical department that practices ``general medical care that examines people'' in the increasingly complex modern medical world. While the specialization of medical care continues to advance, the "fragmentation of medical care" in which patients go back and forth between multiple departments has become a major issue in regional medical care. Especially for the elderly and people with multiple chronic diseases, the complexity of medical care is a serious issue that directly affects their quality of life.

General medicine medicine deals with patients from a holistic perspective, including not only physical symptoms but also psychological and social backgrounds. We value medical care that gently reaches out to the person, not to the disease itself, but to the person's life and values.

We believe that the accumulation of these efforts will create harmony in medical care and spread kindness to the community. In order for Tokyo Police Hospital to continue to be ``the hospital chosen by the community,'' it is essential that each and every employee is facing the same direction and working together. At the core of this, the Department of General Medicine serves as the "first point of contact" for patients across outpatient, inpatient, and emergency departments, contributing to improving the quality of local medical care.

## Treatment contents and characteristics

### We provide peace of mind and trust with our integrated medical care system.

- Outpatient Treatment (First-time Internal Medicine Outpatient Clinic) The Department of General Internal Medicine has a first-time internal medicine outpatient clinic open from Mondays to Saturdays, where we provide a wide range of services for patients with unexplained symptoms, complex health problems, and those who do not know where to go. We have an internal medicine outpatient clinic open from Monday to Saturday. • You don't know which department to go to. • You don't feel well. • You're not sure whether to get a test. • You're experiencing multiple symptoms at the same time. This is the time when you should consult a general medicine physician. Mental fatigue and anxiety in daily life are often hidden behind physical ailments. We don't just look for illnesses, we carefully reconsider the balance of the entire body, and together we think of a path to returning to a healthy daily life. We perform tests and refer patients to other departments as necessary, and provide medical care through multidisciplinary collaboration unique to a general hospital.
- Inpatient treatment If hospitalization is necessary, the general medicine department will provide comprehensive medical care, including physical, psychological, and social aspects. We aim to provide the best medical care by flexibly responding to patients with multiple health problems and cases that are difficult to diagnose.

## Attitudes valued by general medicine medicine | 7 forces that support "medical care that examines people"

The Department of General Medicine has the seven competencies required of a general practitioner as the foundation of its medical practice, and utilizes them in daily medical care. These are not just a collection of technologies, but the very "attitude" we need to stay close to our patients' lives and support their individual health. • Comprehensive ability to respond to a wide range of symptoms • Diagnostic ability to identify unexplained illnesses • Diagnostic ability to respect patients' values ​​• Collaborative ability to collaborate and coordinate with multiple professions • Community power to address local health issues • Specialization to continue improving the quality of medical care • Practical ability across outpatient, emergency, and hospital wards By continuing to refine these skills, we are realizing medical care that supports patients' ``now'' and ``future.''
