## About ophthalmology

The leading cause of blindness in Japan is glaucoma, and the second leading cause is diabetic retinopathy, and our department has long been dedicated to providing detailed treatment for glaucoma. From now on, we will continue to maintain our tradition and prepare for a wide range of other diseases. In particular, we place particular emphasis on various retinal and vitreous diseases such as diabetic retinopathy and retinal detachment, highly difficult cataract surgery and intraocular lens suturing with complications, and secondary and tertiary emergency medical care for eye trauma. We also deal with inflammatory diseases such as external ocular diseases and uveitis, and we collaborate with university hospitals when more specialized medical care is required.

## Main diseases diagnosed at ophthalmology

### 1. vitreoretinal disease

We offer a wide range of treatments for proliferative diabetic retinopathy, proliferative vitreoretinopathy, rhegmatogenous retinal detachment, macular holes, and preretinal membranes, from traditional vitrectomy to the latest microincision vitrectomy surgery (MIVS) and wide-angle systems. Laser treatment, drug treatment, and surgical treatment are used for macular edema. Rhegmatogenous retinal detachment is also amenable to classical retinal replacement surgery.

### 2. crystalline lens disease

We treat everything from normal cataracts to cataracts with complications (lens dislocation, subluxation, weak Chin zone, glaucoma, vitreous retina disease, etc.), and we also handle various complications that occur during cataract surgery.

### 3. glaucoma

We continue to carry on the tradition of our department, including drug treatment, laser treatment, trabeculectomy, trabeculotomy, and angle adhesiolysis for open-angle glaucoma and angle-closure glaucoma.

### 4. eye trauma

Based on our treatment experience of approximately 100 cases of open eye trauma, we provide comprehensive treatment by combining each of the above surgeries.

### 5. others

Uveitis, eyelid disease, pterygium, etc.

## Treatment contents and characteristics

We have five senses: sight, hearing, smell, taste, and touch, and of all these senses, it is said that 80% of the information we receive comes from the senses. Furthermore, in order to maintain normal vision, various parts of the eyeball function in harmony, and if distortion, blood circulation disorder, inflammation, or even anatomical abnormality occurs in any one part, it becomes impossible to maintain the health of the eye, and vision will immediately be affected. There are many testing methods to accurately examine the function and form of each part, and orthoptists are the technicians who accurately perform these tests. Our ophthalmologists use microscopes and ophthalmoscopes to accurately diagnose eye abnormalities and decide on the final treatment plan based on the test results obtained by six highly skilled orthoptists. Depending on the disease, many tests may be required and it may take time to complete the diagnosis and treatment, but we will provide as detailed explanations as possible, avoid falling into the trap of uniform treatment, and strive to meet the needs of each individual patient.

### <Main inspection>

Visual acuity test, refraction test, color vision test, corneal shape analysis, corneal endothelium test, anterior segment three-dimensional image analysis, laser anterior chamber protein cell count test, intraocular pressure test, dynamic quantitative visual field test, static quantitative visual field test, electroretinogram, fundus photography, fundus angiography, ultrasonic tomography (A, B), optical axial length test, fundus three-dimensional image analysis, HRT optic disc analysis, ocular muscle function test, binocular visual function test, strabismus amblyopia test, etc.
