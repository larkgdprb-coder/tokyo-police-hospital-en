## Basic policy

We practice medical care based on evidence (medical evidence proven through clinical trials) for all urinary diseases. We strive to treat the sick, not just treat the disease. In other words, we strive to provide medical care that does not impair the original physical and mental functions of the patient as much as possible.

## Main medical treatment contents

### ①RARP (Robot-Assisted Laparoscopic Prostate Resection), RAPN (Robot-Assisted Laparoscopic Partial Nephrectomy)

We use the surgical support robot da Vinci to perform surgeries for prostate cancer and kidney cancer. It is less invasive than conventional open surgery and endoscopic surgery, and is covered by insurance. It is characterized by less bleeding, less postoperative pain, and fewer complications. Because we can see even the minute structures, it is possible to preserve nerves and normal kidneys. Please consult us as the range that can be preserved varies depending on the case.

### ②MRI/US fusion sniper prostate biopsy

In 2015, prostate cancer became the leading cause of new cases among men in Japan. A definitive diagnosis of prostate cancer is made through a biopsy (pathological examination performed by taking tissue from the prostate). The PSA blood test has been used as a blood marker for early detection for about 20 years, but cases of non-prostate cancer often show abnormal PSA values, and the predictive value for cancer when a biopsy is performed based on an abnormal PSA test alone is only about 30%. At our department, we perform advanced medical care in which, before performing a prostate biopsy, we perform a 3Tesla multiparametric MRI to examine suspected lesions of prostate cancer, and then project the suspected lesions on the MRI image onto an ultrasound image to collect tissue from the same area. Since introducing this method in January 2015, the accuracy rate for prostate cancer has improved to 75%, making it unnecessary to perform excessive prostate biopsies.

### ③Trimodality treatment for high-risk prostate cancer

Prostate cancer diagnosed at an early stage is classified into three groups based on the biological malignancy of the cancer: low, medium, and high risk, and individual treatment strategies are determined. Among these, high-risk cases have a high risk of recurrence and metastasis, and it has been found that the results with single treatment are poor. If total prostatectomy is performed, additional radiation therapy is required with approximately a 50% recurrence rate. Wide excision and extended lymph node dissection have been attempted to improve curability, but no benefit has been found. It is known that the recurrence rate is similarly high when external radiation therapy is applied to the intracranial lymph nodes. Under these circumstances, a new treatment method that has recently attracted attention is a method called Trimodality, which uses three types of treatment from the beginning: endocrine therapy, brachytherapy, and external beam radiation therapy. Our department has actively adopted this method and has achieved better treatment results than manual therapy for high-risk patients.

### ④Laser endoscopic treatment

We perform endoscopic treatment using holmium laser for urinary tract stones and prostate hypertrophy. Regarding urinary blockages, we aim to completely extract them using a rigid/flexible ureteropelvic fiberscope and basket forceps. For prostate hyperplasia, we use the standard endoscopic surgery TURP and laser enucleation HoLEP after carefully examining each case.

### ⑤En bloc resection of non-muscle invasive bladder tumor using fluorescence navigation (PDD-TURBO procedure)

Non-muscle invasive bladder cancer (NMIBC) accounts for approximately 70% of bladder cancers, and the recurrence rate after TURBT is reported to be 31-78%. There is a risk that repeated recurrences may develop into high-grade or invasive cancer, leading to poor prognosis. To prevent recurrence, it is important to 1) completely remove all tumors and 2) avoid dispersing tumor cells during surgery. The cause of ① may be that small lesions or flat lesions (CIS) that are difficult to see remain after TURBT. The cause of ② is that tumor cells are disseminated into the bladder due to the ablation electrode cutting directly into the tumor body. In order to overcome these two points, our department has started a new surgical technique called PDD-TURBO. PDD is an abbreviation for photodynamic diagnosis, and it is a method in which aminolevulinic hydrochloride (AlaglioⓇ), which was listed on the National Health Insurance Price List last year, is taken orally 2 to 3 hours before surgery, and protoporphyrin IX (PPIX), which accumulates in large amounts in tumor cells, is excited with blue light (400-410 nm) to emit red fluorescence. The latest model of the PDD system, manufactured by Germany's Karl Storz, that we purchased this time can instantly convert between white light and blue light, making it possible to skillfully use blue light to assist in diagnosis and white light for excision, while removing any remaining tumor at the resection margin, as well as flat lesions and microscopic lesions that are difficult to see with white light. TURBO is an abbreviation for Transurethral resection of bladder tumor in one piece.With PDD assistance, the resection margins are precisely determined and the tumor is removed in one piece using a special real bipolar electrode. This minimizes tumor cell dissemination during surgery. We are confident that highly accurate diagnosis and reliable tumor removal will improve patient prognosis.

### ⑥Hannah type interstitial cystitis

We perform hydraulic dilatation therapy combined with extensive electrocoagulation for Hanna type interstitial cystitis, which is designated as an incurable disease.
