## About plastic surgery/cosmetic surgery

Our department was the first in Japan to open more than half a century ago, and has produced many plastic and cosmetic surgery instructors from all over the country. We are the first department not only in Japan but also in the world to clinically apply the current state-of-the-art plastic surgery techniques, microvascular surgery (microsurgery) and craniomaxillofacial surgery (craniofacial surgery). We have particularly extensive experience in treating congenital malformations such as cleft lip and palate and craniomaxillofacial bones, and in recent years we have also been actively performing plastic surgery treatments using microsurgery such as facial nerve paralysis and breast reconstruction. In addition, we have opened a cosmetic surgery department and are focusing on comprehensive anti-aging cosmetic medicine.

## Main diseases treated in plastic surgery/cosmetic surgery

The diseases targeted by plastic surgery can be broadly divided into five categories: trauma, congenital malformations, tumors, reconstruction, and aesthetic medicine. Traumatic injuries include facial injuries such as facial bone fractures and facial lacerations, trauma to limbs, and burns.Congenital malformations include cleft lip and palate, craniomaxillofacial bone deformities, auricular deformities, limb deformities, and pectus excavatum.Tumors include benign and malignant skin tumors, hemangiomas, subcutaneous soft tissue tumors, birthmarks, etc. Reconstruction includes breast reconstruction after breast cancer removal, facial paralysis reconstruction, blepharoptosis, skin scar correction, bedsores, diabetic ulcer reconstruction, etc. Cosmetic medicine includes heavy eyelid surgery, facial rhytidation (wrinkle removal and rejuvenation surgery), facial contouring, breast augmentation, external nose surgery, liposuction, age spots and wrinkles treatment, anti-aging medicine, baldness treatment, etc.

## Treatment contents and characteristics

As mentioned above, plastic surgery/cosmetic surgery is a medical department that responds to the needs of patients who want to improve their appearance, make them beautiful, and rejuvenate their appearance, from head to toe, and can be said to be a medical department that improves quality of life (QOL). Most treatments such as trauma, congenital malformations, tumor removal, and reconstruction are covered by health insurance, but cosmetic surgery (cosmetic medicine) is, in principle, free treatment (self-pay treatment). In other words, cosmetic surgery is a field of plastic surgery that is not covered by health insurance. Our department handles approximately 1,500 surgeries per year, including both local and general anesthesia surgeries, making us one of the largest in Japan, and our scope covers a wide range of plastic and cosmetic surgeries (see medical records). Watanabe of our department specializes in craniofacial surgery (craniomaxillofacial osteotomy surgery), which includes the treatment of craniomaxillofacial congenital malformations. Our target diseases are craniofacial bone abnormalities such as cleft lip and palate and Crouzon's disease, and we have one of the most clinical experiences in Japan. We also perform muscle transplantation using microsurgery for facial nerve paralysis, as well as cosmetic surgery treatments such as blepharoptosis and facelifts. Yoshimura specializes in general plastic surgery, breast reconstruction, and cosmetic surgery, and also performs many scar correction surgeries. We have state-of-the-art medical equipment such as Q-switched ruby ​​lasers, phototherapy devices, and photoepilation devices, and we also perform nevus of Ota treatment, tattoo removal, spot treatment, birthmark treatment, and medical hair removal.
