## About the Department of Nephrology and Metabolism

In the Internal Medicine and Renal Metabolism Group, we mainly treat kidney, endocrine, and metabolic diseases. Our department actively promotes collaboration between hospitals and clinics. Hospital collaboration is a system in which not only our department but also a private practice near your home becomes your attending physician, and the attending physician at the hospital and the attending physician at the clinic work together to treat you. This allows us to take advantage of the advantages of both and provide more detailed medical treatment. If you are a patient who already has a regular doctor, please consult with that doctor and bring your referral letter with you, and we will do our best to accommodate you.

## Main diseases treated in the Department of Nephrology and Metabolism

Renal diseases: Renal failure, glomerulonephritis, nephrotic syndrome, etc. Endocrine diseases: Graves' disease, Hashimoto's disease, thyroid tumor (if surgery is required, we will refer you to a specialized hospital), pituitary gland diseases, parathyroid diseases, adrenal gland diseases, etc. Metabolic diseases: diabetes, dyslipidemia, hyperuricemia, metabolic syndrome

## Treatment contents and characteristics

### kidney disease

To determine the cause of hematuria, proteinuria, and impaired renal function, it may be necessary to collect kidney tissue for pathological diagnosis. This test is called a kidney biopsy. If indicated, a kidney biopsy will be performed to confirm the diagnosis and then provide the most appropriate treatment. We also provide detailed management for patients with chronic kidney disease by providing optimal drug therapy and dietary therapy to suppress the decline in renal function.

### endocrine disease

The two major thyroid diseases are Hashimoto's disease and Graves' disease. Hashimoto's disease in particular is a very popular disease that is said to affect one in ten middle-aged women. Our department has a thyroid specialist who actively treats thyroid diseases. We also diagnose and treat diseases of endocrine organs such as the pituitary gland, parathyroid glands, and adrenal glands.

### metabolic disease

Diabetes is now considered a national disease. For type 1 diabetes patients who require insulin injections, we provide detailed advice on injection methods, blood sugar measurements, and daily life, and if necessary, we also use a portable insulin pump called CSII to help patients better control their diabetes. Recently, new drugs have been released one after another for type 2 diabetes, which is strongly influenced by lifestyle habits, and we provide treatment while considering the best option depending on the patient's condition. However, before that, there is a reality that although dietary therapy is important, it is generally not given much guidance. In our department, we actively provide guidance from nutritionists even in outpatient settings, and we strive to provide basic treatment that does not just rely on drugs. Regarding dyslipidemia and hyperuricemia, we first provide lifestyle guidance and consider medication according to the patient's condition.
