## About surgery

The department of surgery covers a wide range of medical fields, including gastrointestinal, peripheral blood vessels, mammary glands, and respiratory organs. With the motto of "speedy medical treatment" and "medical treatment that emphasizes quality of life," we strive every day to treat each patient with care, from diagnosis to surgery, chemotherapy, and palliative care.

## Main diseases treated in surgery

### digestive system

Stomach cancer/esophageal cancer, colorectal cancer, liver/bile/pancreatic cancer, cholelithiasis, hemorrhoid disease, etc.

### mammary gland

Breast cancer, benign mammary tumor, mastopathy, etc.

### peripheral blood vessels

Abdominal aorta, arteriosclerosis obliterans, varicose veins, deep vein thrombosis, etc.

### others

Lung cancer, spontaneous pneumothorax, inguinal hernia, etc.

### Gastrointestinal surgery

Among malignant diseases, colorectal cancer surgery was the most common at 119 cases per year, and including 25 cases of early-stage cancer treated endoscopically, the number of cases of colorectal cancer treated was 144 cases per year. Colorectal cancer is rapidly increasing in Japan, but it is no exaggeration to say that death from colorectal cancer can be 100% avoided through regular examinations. Our department is actively working on the early detection of colorectal cancer, and our staff has a track record of performing over 3,000 colonoscopies a year. In recent years, the indications for ESD (endoscopic submucosal isolation) for early-stage gastric cancer have expanded, and the number of gastric cancer surgeries itself has decreased, but last year there were 61 gastric cancer surgeries performed in our department. For early gastric cancer, the suitability of ESD is first determined by a gastroenterologist, and surgical resection is performed only in cases where surgery is deemed necessary. Among benign diseases, hernia is the most common, followed by gallbladder diseases (stones, polyps, adenomyosis, etc.). More than 90% of cholecystectomies are performed laparoscopically.

### peripheral vascular surgery

Regarding vascular surgery, we actively incorporate cutting-edge treatment methods and aim to provide high-level medical care. Depending on the disease, we also collaborate with radiology, cardiovascular medicine, dermatology, and plastic surgery to provide diagnosis and treatment. Arterial diseases include abdominal aortic aneurysm, arteriosclerosis obliterans of the lower extremities, and carotid artery stenosis. There are options for endovascular treatment, surgical treatment, and drug therapy for each type of treatment, but we select the treatment that is considered optimal for each case after considering the advantages and disadvantages of each. If we determine that a stent graft is necessary for an abdominal aortic aneurysm, we will refer you to our affiliated university hospital. Varicose veins in the lower extremities are diagnosed using ultrasound, and the surgical incisions can now be made much smaller than in the past. In principle, surgery is performed under general anesthesia under the supervision of an anesthesiologist in order to reduce pain and ensure safety. The schedule is for patients to be admitted to the hospital in the morning the day before surgery and discharged the day after surgery. We do not currently offer laser treatment for varicose veins, but we are planning to introduce it in the future. Regarding dialysis shunts, in addition to creating arteriovenous shunts using autologous veins or artificial blood vessels, we also perform endovascular treatment (shunt PTA) for shunt problems, with good results. If you visit a doctor because it is difficult to puncture or you can't get enough flow, if it's in the early stages, we can immediately confirm the lesion with vascular ultrasound and then proceed with endovascular treatment. We believe that if we can respond early, it will be possible to use existing shunts for as long as possible.

### breast surgery

At our hospital, breast cancer diagnosis (image diagnostics such as mammography, ultrasound, and MRI, and pathological tests such as cytology and histology) and treatment (surgery, radiation therapy, and drug therapy) are performed by a breast team consisting of a surgeon and other doctors, nurses, and technicians from different fields. We also work closely with plastic surgeons regarding cosmetic surgery, including post-mastectomy reconstruction. Breast cancer has increased rapidly in Japan in recent years, and it is said that approximately 1 in 12 Japanese women will develop breast cancer. In 2019, we performed 155 surgeries for breast cancer and malignant breast tumors, and 27 surgeries for benign tumors. Of the breast cancer surgeries, 89 were conservative surgeries and 66 were total mastectomies (including 6 primary reconstructions performed at the same time as total mastectomy). Sentinel lymph node biopsy was performed in 120 cases using a combination of dye method and RI (radioisotope) method, of which axillary lymph node dissection was omitted in 105 cases. Axillary lymph node dissection was performed in 41 cases (including after sentinel lymph node biopsy). Early detection and early treatment are important for breast cancer, just like stomach cancer and colorectal cancer. If you experience symptoms such as a breast lump, pain, or discharge from the nipple, we recommend that you visit our department as soon as possible.

◇ We do not perform breast cancer screenings for people who do not have symptoms. We only provide treatment for those who require detailed examinations at our clinic or other facilities, those who require treatment or follow-up observation, and those who have symptoms. Please note that mammary gland outpatient clinics are not available on Wednesday mornings and Saturday afternoons.
