## About anesthesiology

Our hospital is certified by the Japanese Society of Anesthesiology, and our Japanese Society of Anesthesiology certified instructors, specialists, board-certified physicians, and late-stage trainees, all of whom are above the ranks of certified anesthesiologists certified by the Ministry of Health, Labor and Welfare, provide safe, high-quality anesthesia. At the Pain Clinic Outpatient Clinic, specialists certified by the Japan Society of Pain Clinics provide medical treatment for people suffering from various types of pain.

## anesthesia in the operating room

### For those who can undergo surgery

Those who undergo surgery at our hospital will undergo several preoperative tests and then visit the anesthesiology outpatient department. At the anesthesiology outpatient clinic, we will ask you about your past medical history, the diseases you are currently treating, and your daily life, and we will explain the anesthesia method, issues with systemic management, and possible complications. Surgery puts a lot of stress on your body, but the anesthesiologist will do everything in his power to ensure that your surgery is completed safely.

You may be worried about various things, but it is important to have a correct understanding of surgery and anesthesia. Please take a look at this video to take a step back from thinking, ``Anesthesia is kind of scary.''

### Postoperative pain management

For surgeries where post-operative pain is expected to be relatively strong, the following pain countermeasures are implemented in addition to general anesthesia. Epidural anesthesia Peripheral nerve block ivPCA (self-administered intravenous administration of analgesics)

### Collaboration with intensive care units in high-risk cases

For major surgeries that are highly difficult and invasive, or surgeries for patients with major pre-existing medical conditions, we hold detailed conferences with the attending physician and related departments before the surgery. We will then take all possible measures to deal with expected complications and various possible situations, and if necessary, provide full-body care in the intensive care unit even after surgery. The existence of a backup intensive care unit is an extremely important factor in safely performing surgery.

## Pain clinic outpatient clinic

### Main diseases treated at the anesthesiology department (pain clinic)

Herpes zoster, postherpetic neuralgia, lower back and lower limb pain, neck, shoulder and arm pain, trigeminal neuralgia, phantom limb pain, CRPS cancer pain (in-hospital referral only)

At our outpatient pain clinic, we treat intractable pain using a combination of nerve block therapies such as stellate ganglion blocks, epidural blocks, brachial plexus blocks, and trigeminal nerve blocks, physical therapy using linearly polarized near-infrared therapy (Superlyzer), and drug therapy using analgesics, anticonvulsants, and opioids.
