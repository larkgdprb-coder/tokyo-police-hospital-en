## Treatment contents and characteristics

### Psychotherapy/counseling consultation and introduction

In principle, our goal is to improve symptoms with drugs and provide guidance on environmental adjustment so that patients can return to their normal lives as quickly as possible. However, behind the various symptoms, there may be influences such as the patient's own mental inconvenience, trauma, family relationships, etc. In most cases, the same pattern repeats several times, and in such cases, it may be difficult to deal with the medical treatment covered by insurance alone. There are cases where drug treatment is better than psychotherapy alone, and I think it is difficult to make a decision on your own. We are able to make such judgments and provide advice by having your first medical examination covered by insurance. We will introduce paid counseling services (psychoanalytic psychotherapy, cognitive behavioral therapy, family therapy, EMDR, etc.) as needed.

### Depression/Bipolar disorder

If symptoms such as ``feeling depressed,'' ``not having any interest in anything,'' or ``lack of motivation'' persist for more than a few weeks, it may be due to so-called ``depression,'' but it may also be ``bipolar affective disorder,'' in which the person repeatedly experiences high and low moods, or ``adjustment disorder,'' in which mental and physical discomfort occurs in response to strong stress. Depression can also be caused by physical illness. Although the symptoms may be similar, treatment methods differ depending on the cause, so we will conduct a careful interview and tests at your first visit to diagnose the cause early and provide appropriate treatment. In addition to medication treatment, if the attending physician deems it necessary, a psychologist will also conduct psychological tests and cognitive behavioral therapy. If your depression does not get better, you can reconsider the diagnosis by using optical topography as an auxiliary diagnostic test.

### Panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, generalized anxiety disorder

For panic disorder, which is accompanied by strong anxiety caused by sudden onset of palpitations, shortness of breath, and trembling, social anxiety disorder, which manifests as blushing, trembling, and palpitations due to fear of failure in front of others, and generalized anxiety disorder, in which constant vague anxiety interferes with work and family life, we provide medication and, if a doctor deems it necessary, cognitive behavioral therapy by a psychologist.

### schizophrenia

Symptoms include not trusting those around you, hearing voices you can't hear, being watched, and being unkind to people around you, but many people also suffer from cognitive dysfunction, such as decreased attention span, lack of control, and poor memory. Even if the hallucinations and delusions are subdued by medication, the patient may lack motivation and tend to stay at home, requiring rehabilitation in order to work or participate in society. We can collaborate with various organizations and provide advice regarding the use of social resources such as day care for rehabilitation, employment continuity support offices, employment transition support facilities, and Hello Work.

### Epilepsy specialist outpatient clinic

Epilepsy is a disease that causes seizures such as whole body convulsions, loss of consciousness, and spontaneous body movements due to excessive synchronization of nerve cells in the brain. In our department, epilepsy specialists diagnose and treat epilepsy. We will conduct a detailed interview and confirm the situation using electroencephalogram tests and head images. Regardless of the presence or absence of psychiatric symptoms, we can adjust antiepileptic seizure medications and provide lifestyle advice, and if psychiatric symptoms coexist, we can also provide treatment. If you have intractable epilepsy or are having difficulty diagnosing it, we will refer you to an epilepsy center and work with you. Depending on your condition, we may recommend that you visit another hospital in advance. note that.

### Consultation liaison

This is a medical service for people admitted to our hospital. We provide psychological support (consultation and liaison). In order to ensure that you can spend your time in the hospital with peace of mind, we may use tranquilizers if you are mentally unstable, adjust your sleeping pills, or provide counseling with a psychologist at the same time.

### palliative care

In order to alleviate the physical pain and mental hardship caused by cancer, etc., we provide mental support to those who request it while they are hospitalized at our hospital as a member of the palliative care team.

### dementia

Currently, we do not treat patients without a referral letter. In addition to psychological tests (cognitive function tests) to check the degree of forgetfulness, CT/MRI (to check the shape of the brain), and imaging tests such as SPECT (to check the blood flow in the brain), MIBG scintigraphy and DaT scan to differentiate Lewy body dementia are performed as needed. For continued hospital visits, please contact your family medical institution.

### Note) Things that our hospital's psychiatry department cannot do

- * m-ECT (modified electroconvulsive therapy) and rTMS (repetitive transcranial stimulation therapy), which are treatments for depression, are not available.
- *We cannot prescribe the ADHD medications Concerta and Vyvanse, and the narcolepsy medications Ritalin and Modiodal. Selincro, a drug used to reduce alcoholism, cannot be prescribed.
- * Risperdal Consta and Zeprion, which are long-acting injections for schizophrenia, cannot be injected. Abilify LAI, Halomance, and Fludecasine have a track record of use.
- *We cannot diagnose or treat mental illnesses such as autism spectrum disorder, ADHD, etc. in children of infancy to elementary and junior high school age.
- * Psychiatric inpatient treatment is not available. If hospitalization is necessary, we will refer you to other hospitals (mainly the University of Tokyo Hospital, Teikyo University Hospital, Kichijoji Hospital, Inokashira Hospital, Jiundo Hospital, Yowa Hospital, Tokyo Musashino Hospital, etc.).
- *Emergency response at night and on holidays is not possible.
- *Overnight hospitalization tests for sleep apnea syndrome are not available. (We also perform simple tests that can be done at home)
- *We do not have treatment programs for alcoholism or drug addiction. If necessary, we will refer you to nearby specialized facilities (Jiyu Clinic, Apari Clinic, Inokashira Hospital, Tokyo Alcohol Center, etc.).
