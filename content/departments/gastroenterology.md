## About gastroenterology

Gastroenterology treats diseases of the gastrointestinal tract such as the esophagus, stomach, and intestines, as well as diseases of real organs such as the liver, bile tract, and pancreas. We diagnose and treat cancers of various organs, intractable diseases (inflammatory bowel disease, hepatitis, pancreatitis), and other acute and chronic diseases. We also accept detailed examinations of medical examination results and consultations regarding pylori infection.

In terms of treatment, the gastroenterology department's motto is to "cure without cutting" from an internal medicine perspective, and by introducing the latest medical technology, we aim to cure diseases with a method that minimizes the physical, time, and financial burden on patients. On the other hand, depending on the medical condition, we will work with doctors in the surgery and radiology departments to consult on diagnosis and treatment policies, so that we can always offer the best treatment to the patient.

## Main diseases treated in gastroenterology

- Gastrointestinal diseases Esophagitis, esophageal cancer, esophageal varices, reflux esophagitis, gastritis, gastric ulcer, gastric cancer, gastric polyp, functional gastroenteropathy, duodenal ulcer, colon polyp, colon cancer, ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease, acute gastroenteritis, intestinal obstruction, colon diverticulitis, colon diverticulum bleeding
- Liver disease Chronic hepatitis, fatty liver
- Biliary/pancreatic diseases Gallbladder stones, bile duct stones, cholecystitis, cholangitis, gallbladder cancer, bile duct cancer, autoimmune pancreatitis, acute pancreatitis, chronic pancreatitis, pancreatic cancer, pancreatic cyst, obstructive jaundice

## Treatment contents and characteristics

### Main inspection

Upper gastrointestinal endoscopy (gastroscope): esophagus, stomach, duodenum. Important for early cancer diagnosis. In addition to conventional oral endoscopy, our hospital also uses transnasal endoscopy. Please contact us if you are interested.

Lower gastrointestinal endoscopy (colon camera): Important for diagnosis and treatment of colon cancer and polyps. Chronic diarrhea and constipation may be caused by an unexpected disease. We strive to perform colonoscopies with minimal pain through careful operation of the endoscope. Abdominal ultrasound (echo): Observes the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, kidneys, spleen, and lymph nodes.

Endoscopic ultrasound: An endoscope is used to examine the details of lesions in the gastrointestinal tract, pancreas, gallbladder, etc. using ultrasound.

### Treatment of major diseases

- [Gastrointestinal diseases] Esophageal cancer, stomach cancer, colorectal cancer: Very early gastrointestinal cancers are removed using an endoscope (endoscopic mucosal resection/endoscopic submucosal dissection). If the cancer cannot be removed with an endoscope, we will refer you to a surgeon and aim to completely cure the cancer through open surgery. If surgery is difficult or is not desired, anticancer drug treatment or radiation therapy will be performed. Gastric ulcer/duodenal ulcer: As a general rule, bleeding ulcers are treated with an endoscope to stop the bleeding. Additionally, once the ulcer has subsided, Helicobacter pylori will be eradicated to prevent recurrence. Chronic gastritis: The main cause is Helicobacter pylori infection. It is known to be associated with gastric cancer, and regular endoscopy is recommended for early detection. Helicobacter pylori eradication for chronic gastritis can be performed under health insurance only if gastritis has been definitively diagnosed by endoscopy. Please consult the outpatient department for details. Inflammatory bowel disease: Intractable diseases include ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease, and Behcet's disease. Dietary therapy and drug therapy will be performed. For intractable cases, we will consider the latest treatments using leukocyte depletion therapy and molecular target drugs. Functional gastrointestinal disorders: Broadly divided into gastroesophageal reflux disease, functional gastroenteropathy, and irritable bowel syndrome. There are many patients who are troubled by symptoms even though they have been told that there is no abnormality in the test. We recommend treatments such as dietary therapy and drug therapy that suit the patient.
- [Liver disease] Chronic hepatitis: We aim to control hepatitis with antiviral drugs. Even when strong drugs cannot be used due to side effects, we can suppress the progression of the disease by suppressing inflammation.
- [Biliary/pancreatic disease] Biliary tract cancer/pancreatic cancer: If accompanied by jaundice, jaundice is treated using an endoscope. Even if surgical removal is not possible, treatment with anticancer drugs can suppress the progression of the cancer. Common bile duct stones: Stones are removed using an endoscope without cutting into the abdomen. Cholangitis/Cholecystitis: Infection is controlled by endoscopic or percutaneous drainage. If cholelithiasis is the cause, treatment for the cause will be considered.
