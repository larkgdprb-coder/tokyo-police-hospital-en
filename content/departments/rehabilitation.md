## About rehabilitation department

This is a medical department that provides medical treatment and therapeutic training for people who have motor dysfunction or higher brain dysfunction due to illness or injury. We help patients recover their lost functions, provide treatment and training to maximize their remaining abilities, and help patients return to their homes and society. The treatment method consists of three departments: physical therapy, which aims to restore basic movement and mobility abilities; occupational therapy, which aims to restore daily living abilities and social adaptability; and speech-language therapy, which aims to restore swallowing, language, and cognitive abilities. Under the supervision of a rehabilitation doctor, treatment is carried out from a specialized standpoint in order to help patients perform various practical activities in daily life.

## Main diseases treated in the rehabilitation department

We provide rehabilitation for the diseases listed below in accordance with facility standards established by the Ministry of Health, Labor and Welfare.

### Ⅰ Cerebrovascular disease rehabilitation (I)

① Patients with cerebral infarction, cerebral hemorrhage, subarachnoid hemorrhage, or other acute-onset cerebrovascular diseases, or those who have undergone surgery. ② Patients with brain tumors, brain abscesses, spinal cord injuries, spinal cord tumors, or other acute-onset central nervous diseases, or those who have undergone surgery. ③ Patients with polyneuritis, multiple sclerosis, peripheral neuropathy, or other neurological diseases. ④ Patients with Parkinson's disease, spinocerebellar degeneration, or other chronic neuromuscular diseases. ⑤ Patients with aphasia, agnosia and apraxia, and higher brain dysfunction

### Ⅱ Locomotor rehabilitation (I)

① Patients with complex injuries to the upper and lower limbs, quadriplegia due to spinal injury, or other acute onset musculoskeletal diseases, or those who have undergone surgery ② Patients with degenerative diseases of the joints, inflammatory diseases of the joints, joint contractures due to burn scars, or other chronic musculoskeletal diseases that have reduced motor function and daily living ability to a certain degree or more

### Ⅲ Respiratory rehabilitation (I)

① Patients with pneumonia, atelectasis, or other acute-onset respiratory diseases ② Patients with lung tumors, chest trauma, or other respiratory diseases, or those who have undergone surgery for them ③ Patients with severe breathing difficulties of a certain degree or more or decreased ability to live a daily life due to chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, bronchial asthma, interstitial pneumonia, or other chronic respiratory diseases ④ Patients who require respiratory function training before and after surgery for esophageal cancer, stomach cancer, liver cancer, throat/larynx cancer, etc.

### Ⅳ Disuse syndrome rehabilitation (I)

① Patients with disuse syndrome due to bed rest due to acute illness, etc., who have a certain degree of decline in basic movement ability, applied movement ability, speech and hearing ability, and daily living ability.

### Ⅴ Cardiovascular disease rehabilitation (I)

① Patients with acute myocardial infarction, angina pectoris attack, or other acute onset cardiovascular or macrovascular disease, or patients who have undergone surgery for the same. ② Patients who have a certain degree or more of decreased respiratory circulatory function and impaired daily life due to chronic heart failure, peripheral arterial occlusive disease, or other chronic cardiovascular disease.

### Ⅵ Cancer patient rehabilitation (I)

① Patients who are cancer patients who are scheduled to receive or have undergone surgery, chemotherapy (limited to those where bone marrow suppression is expected), radiation therapy, or hematopoietic stem cell transplantation while hospitalized for cancer treatment. ② Patients with advanced or terminal cancer who are undergoing treatment for the purpose of palliative care and who require rehabilitation to return home due to exacerbation of symptoms while hospitalized.

## Treatment contents and characteristics

Our hospital's rehabilitation department is staffed by doctors, physical therapists, occupational therapists, and speech-language pathologists, and has a system that allows for comprehensive rehabilitation. We provide rehabilitation for a wide range of illnesses, including stroke and other cerebrovascular diseases, musculoskeletal diseases such as trauma and diseases of bones, joints, ligaments, and spine, respiratory diseases, cardiovascular diseases, disuse syndrome, and cancer patients, from the hyperacute stage to the recovery stage. During the acute phase, we have a system in place that allows us to manage risks in collaboration with doctors from each department and start rehabilitation from an early stage. We also have a 30-bed convalescent rehabilitation ward, which provides intensive inpatient rehabilitation for patients who aim to return home. We have a large training room (448.63 m2 in total) equipped with the latest training equipment and testing equipment for swallowing evaluation.
