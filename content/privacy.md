## Privacy Policy

Tokyo Police Hospital (*Tōkyō Keisatsu Byōin*), operated by the General Foundation Jikkei-kai, is committed to protecting the personal information of all patients, families, staff, and visitors in accordance with the **Act on the Protection of Personal Information** (*Kojin Jōhō Hogo Hō*) and all applicable Japanese laws and regulations.

## 1. Information We Collect

In the course of providing medical care, we collect the following types of personal information:

- Name, date of birth, gender, and contact details
- Health insurance information and insurance card numbers
- Medical history, diagnosis, treatment records, and test results
- Prescription and medication records
- Images (X-rays, CT, MRI, photographs)
- Billing and payment information
- Information provided voluntarily by patients or their representatives

## 2. Purpose of Use

Personal information collected by Tokyo Police Hospital is used for the following purposes:

1. **Provision of medical care** — Diagnosis, treatment, nursing, pharmacy, and rehabilitation services
2. **Medical administration** — Scheduling, billing, insurance claims, and medical records management
3. **Communication** — Contacting patients, families, and referring physicians regarding care
4. **Quality improvement** — Analysis of care outcomes, patient safety reviews, and quality programs (using anonymized or de-identified data wherever possible)
5. **Medical education and research** — Training of medical students and residents, and approved research (with anonymization or explicit consent)
6. **Legal and regulatory compliance** — Reporting required by law

## 3. Disclosure to Third Parties

We will not disclose personal information to third parties without the patient's consent, **except in the following cases**:

- When required by law (e.g., public health reporting, court order)
- When sharing with referring physicians or healthcare institutions for continuity of care (within the scope of medical care)
- When sharing with emergency services in a life-threatening situation
- When sharing with insurance companies for billing purposes (within the scope of the patient's insurance contract)

## 4. Security Measures

We implement strict technical and organizational measures to protect personal information from unauthorized access, loss, alteration, or disclosure, including:

- Secure electronic medical records with access controls
- Encrypted data transmission
- Physical security of paper records
- Regular staff training on privacy and information security
- Audit logging of access to medical records

## 5. Patient Rights

Patients have the right to:

- **Access** their own personal information held by the hospital
- **Request corrections** to inaccurate or incomplete information
- **Request suspension of use** of their information in specific circumstances
- **Inquire** about how their information is being used

To exercise these rights, please contact the Privacy Office (see below).

## 6. Use of Anonymized Data

Anonymized or de-identified medical data may be used for research, quality improvement, and public health purposes without individual consent, where it is not possible to identify any individual from the data.

## 7. Website & Cookie Information

This website does not collect personally identifiable information through cookies or tracking technologies beyond standard web server access logs (IP address, browser type, date/time of visit). We do not sell or share web access data with third parties.
