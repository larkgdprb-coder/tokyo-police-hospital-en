## How to calculate hospitalization medical expenses

From July 1, 2008, our hospital has introduced the "Depending on Payment Method (DPC)" in accordance with the guidance of the Ministry of Health, Labor and Welfare. Instead of the ``piece-rate payment method,'' which calculated for each medical procedure such as medicines and tests, we will now use the ``comprehensive payment method,'' which calculates a fixed amount of medical expenses per day according to the disease name and treatment content (diagnosis group classification). *Meal expenses will be billed as usual.

## What is DPC (Diagnosis Procedure Combination)?

This method calculates medical expenses by combining comprehensive evaluation (medication fees, injection fees, hospitalization fees, etc.) based on a fixed amount of points per day determined by the Ministry of Health, Labor and Welfare, and performance evaluation (surgery fees, anesthesia fees, etc.), depending on whether or not medical procedures such as surgery are required based on the patient's disease name and symptoms.

## Billing method

### Billing on the day of discharge

- Medical costs will be calculated based on the patient's applicable diagnostic group classification.
- The bill will be delivered to your hospital room around 10am.

### Billing during hospitalization (regular billing)

- Medical costs will be calculated based on the patient's applicable diagnostic group classification.
- Payment ends at the end of the month and will be billed once a month.
- A bill will be delivered to your hospital room around the 10th of each month.
