## About visitation

| Visiting hours | Every day *Including Sundays and holidays | 15:00-19:00 (Visitors can meet once a day for no more than 1 hour) |
| --- | --- | --- |




- When visiting, please fill in the name of the visitor at the information desk. The number of visitors is limited to 3 people. (Ages 12 and over) During your visit, please refrain from doing anything that may disturb other patients (such as eating, drinking, or making loud noises in the hospital room or lounge). Please wear a mask during your visit.







## Family visitation on the day of surgery




- On the day of your surgery, you will be able to meet with us for around 15 minutes before and after surgery, even outside of the visiting hours set by our hospital. After-hours visitors are limited to two people, and as a general rule, visitors are allowed until 7:00 p.m. If you wish to visit, please register at the general information desk on the 1st floor, receive a surgical visitation badge, go to the relevant ward, and speak to the staff station.




*Please do not enter the private room directly.




- The start time of the surgery may be moved earlier due to circumstances on the day of the surgery. Please note that in this case, we may not be able to meet you before surgery.




## Notes on visiting people




- In order to protect hospitalized patients from infection, as a general rule, we do not allow visitors to those who are not feeling well, such as those with a fever, cough, or diarrhea.
- Please wash your hands with alcohol-based hand sanitizer when entering and exiting the hospital or patient room, and after using the restroom.
- During times when infectious diseases such as the new coronavirus are prevalent, please wear a mask even if you have no symptoms. (Please prepare your own mask. You can also purchase one at the hospital.) Visits may also be restricted. In that case, we will post it on our website homepage, so please check it.
- We do not accept fresh flowers for sympathy, as they can become a breeding ground for infectious bacteria regardless of whether they have roots or soil, and to prevent accidents and contamination of the treatment environment due to falling vases.
- Please refrain from eating in your hospital room or lounge.




## About the sympathy email




Tokyo Police Hospital offers a service that sends condolence emails free of charge to hospitalized patients. The email you sent will be printed and delivered to each patient. This is a service that both patients and visitors will appreciate. If you would like to use this service, please read the information below, and once you understand the content, please click the "Click here for sympathy email" button.






In the following cases, we may not be able to handle your order.




- If the content does not offer condolences or encouragement, or if the content is completely unrelated; If the inpatient cannot be identified due to incomplete input; If the patient refuses to receive the email (the patient cannot confirm the sender); If the patient has already been discharged from the hospital. Please note that we may not be able to respond to emails that are on holidays or are urgent.




- We will strictly maintain the privacy of email content. (The contents of your email will not be viewed by anyone other than the person in charge.)
