## Visiting Hours

| | |
|---|---|
| **Visiting Hours** | 15:00 – 19:00 (daily, including weekends and holidays) |
| **Maximum Visit Duration** | 1 hour per visit |
| **Maximum Number of Visitors** | Up to 3 visitors at one time |
| **Minimum Age for Visitors** | 12 years old and above |

We appreciate your understanding and cooperation in following these rules to protect the health and recovery of all patients.

## General Rules for Visitors

1. **Masks are required** at all times within the hospital building.
2. **Hand hygiene** – please use the hand sanitizer provided at the ward entrance before and after visiting.
3. **No food or beverages** may be brought into patient rooms unless specifically approved by the attending physician or dietitian.
4. **Please speak quietly** and be mindful of other patients resting nearby.
5. **Do not enter other patients' rooms**.
6. **Photography and video recording** of other patients or staff is strictly prohibited.

## Restrictions on Cut Flowers

To protect patients with compromised immune systems, the following restrictions apply:

- **Cut flowers are not permitted** in wards where immunocompromised patients are treated (e.g., hematology, post-transplant wards).
- Potted plants with soil are generally **not permitted** throughout the hospital, as soil can harbor bacteria and fungi.
- Please check with the nursing staff before bringing flowers.

## Visiting During Infectious Disease Outbreaks

During periods of elevated infection risk (e.g., influenza season, COVID-19 outbreaks), visiting restrictions may be tightened or suspended entirely. Please check the hospital's latest notices before visiting.

## Cannot Visit in Person?

If you are unable to visit in person, you can send your thoughts and support through our **Get Well Email Service**. Messages sent to hospitalized patients are printed and delivered to them.

See the [Get Well Email Service]({{< relref "mail" >}}) page for details.

## Special Circumstances

If you require a visit outside of standard hours (e.g., for end-of-life care, critical condition), please consult with the ward nurse or a social worker. Arrangements may be made on a case-by-case basis.
