## Repeat Visit Information

For patients who have previously been seen at Tokyo Police Hospital, please follow the procedures below when scheduling and attending your follow-up visits.

## Booking a Repeat Appointment

### Repeat Appointment Center

| | |
|---|---|
| **Phone** | 03-5343-5620 / 03-5343-5621 |
| **Available Hours** | Monday – Saturday, 8:15 – 17:00 |

You can schedule, change, or cancel appointments by telephone. Please have your patient ID number ready when calling.

### Online Appointment Booking

Repeat appointments may also be made online via the hospital's patient portal. Please ask reception for details.

## Re-examination Surcharge

A **re-examination surcharge of ¥3,300** (tax included) may be applied in certain cases, including:

- Patients who have not visited the hospital for more than **3 months** since their last appointment
- Patients who visit without a scheduled appointment when appointment-only system is in place
- Patients returning from a referral to another facility without a return referral letter

This surcharge supports the government policy encouraging patients to use local clinics for routine and ongoing care, reserving hospital capacity for complex cases.

## On the Day of Your Visit

1. Check in at the **Repeat Visit Reception Counter** (1st Floor) or use the **self-check-in kiosk**.
2. Verify your insurance information if your insurance has changed.
3. Pay any outstanding balance from your previous visit if prompted.
4. Proceed to the waiting area for your department.

## Prescription and Medication Pickup

- Prescriptions are issued after your consultation.
- You may fill your prescription at the **hospital pharmacy** (1F) or at any pharmacy of your choice outside the hospital.
- If you have questions about your medications, please consult a pharmacist.

## Notes

- Afternoon clinic hours are by **appointment only** (except Pediatrics).
- Please bring your **patient registration card** and **insurance card** to every visit.
- If you need to cancel, please call as early as possible to allow your slot to be offered to other patients.
