## Referral Letters (Shōkaijō)

A **referral letter** (*shōkaijō*, 紹介状) is a document issued by your regular physician, clinic, or another hospital that introduces you to a specialist at Tokyo Police Hospital. We strongly encourage all new patients to obtain a referral letter before visiting.

## Benefits of Bringing a Referral Letter

- **No initial consultation surcharge**: Patients with a referral letter are **exempt** from the ¥7,700 surcharge.
- **Faster and more efficient care**: The referring physician provides relevant medical history, test results, and diagnosis notes, allowing our specialists to assess your condition more quickly.
- **Continuity of care**: After your treatment at our hospital, a report will be sent back to your referring physician, ensuring coordinated care.

## How to Obtain a Referral Letter

1. Visit your regular clinic or primary care physician.
2. Explain that you wish to be referred to Tokyo Police Hospital and the specialty you need.
3. Your doctor will prepare a referral letter for you.
4. Bring the sealed referral letter to our reception on the day of your visit.

## Return Referrals

After your treatment or upon stable condition, we may issue a **return referral letter** to send you back to your referring physician for continued care. This promotes the appropriate use of hospital resources and ensures your primary care physician remains informed.

## Without a Referral Letter

If you do not have a referral letter, you may still visit our outpatient clinic. However, a surcharge of **¥7,700** (tax included) will be added to your bill. Please see [First Visit]({{< relref "first-visit" >}}) for details on surcharge exemptions.
