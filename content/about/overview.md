## Historical Timeline

| Year | Event |
|---|---|
| **1920** | Planning began for a dedicated hospital for the Tokyo Metropolitan Police Department |
| **1929** | Tokyo Police Hospital officially founded (March 18) in Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo |
| **1945** | Hospital survived World War II; continues operations |
| **1960s–70s** | Expansion of clinical departments and nursing staff |
| **1980s** | Establishment of specialized centers; adoption of new diagnostic technologies |
| **1990s** | Introduction of advanced imaging equipment (MRI, CT); expanded emergency services |
| **2000** | Began transition planning for new facility |
| **2008** | Relocation to current site in Nakano-ku; new 9-story building opened |
| **2010** | Received accreditation from the Japan Council for Quality Health Care (JCQHC) |
| **2015** | Introduction of da Vinci robotic surgical system |
| **2018** | Designated as Cancer Designated Hospital |
| **2020** | Establishment of COVID-19 response protocols; continues to serve community through pandemic |
| **2023** | Continued quality improvement; updated DPC classification; expansion of robotic surgery program |

## Hospital Designation & Certifications

Tokyo Police Hospital holds numerous official designations:

- **Acute Emergency Hospital** (Level 2 Emergency)
- **DPC Hospital** (Standard DPC Group)
- **Cancer Designated Hospital**
- **Stroke Center**
- **Disaster Medical Assistance Team (DMAT) Base Hospital**
- **JCQHC-Accredited Hospital**
- **Referral-Focused Medical Institution**
