## Next-Generation Support Action Plan

Tokyo Police Hospital has formulated an **Action Plan for the Promotion of Measures to Support the Development of the Next Generation** in accordance with the Act on Advancement of Measures to Support Raising Next-Generation Children (次世代育成支援対策推進法).

## Plan Period

**April 1, 2025 – March 31, 2030** (5 years)

## Background

Creating a workplace that supports employees in balancing their professional responsibilities with family life — including raising children and caring for elderly relatives — is essential for the long-term sustainability of our workforce and the quality of care we provide to patients.

We recognize that a diverse, well-supported workforce is the foundation of a high-quality hospital. This plan sets out concrete, measurable goals to improve work-life balance for all employees.

## Goals and Measures

### Goal 1: Increase Male Employee Parental Leave Rate to 70% or Higher

**Current Status:** As of fiscal year 2024, the male parental leave rate at our hospital is below the national healthcare industry average.

**Target:** Achieve a male parental leave utilization rate of **70% or higher** by March 2030.

**Measures:**
- Active promotion of paternity leave entitlements to all male employees
- Introduction of a "Paternity Leave Declaration" system at the time a birth is announced
- Designation of a department-level supervisor responsible for facilitating leave requests
- Reduction of barriers (workload distribution, substitute staffing) to make leave practically accessible
- Regular reporting and monitoring of parental leave utilization rates

### Goal 2: Reduce the Proportion of Employees Working Overtime Exceeding 45 Hours/Month by 10%

**Current Status:** A proportion of employees — particularly in clinical departments — regularly work overtime beyond 45 hours per month.

**Target:** Reduce the proportion of employees working more than 45 hours of overtime per month by **10 percentage points** compared to the baseline.

**Measures:**
- Review and optimize shift scheduling across departments
- Introduce electronic task management tools to improve efficiency
- Regular review of workload distribution within teams
- Promotion of annual leave utilization (targeting full utilization of statutory leave)
- Senior management commitment to overtime reduction and visible leadership on this issue

## Monitoring and Review

Progress toward these goals will be reviewed **annually** by the hospital's HR and Workplace Environment Committee. Results will be reported to the relevant labor-management consultative body and published internally.

## Our Commitment

We are committed to building a workplace where every employee — regardless of gender, parental status, or caregiving responsibilities — can thrive professionally while maintaining a fulfilling personal life. A healthy, motivated workforce is the foundation of excellent patient care.
