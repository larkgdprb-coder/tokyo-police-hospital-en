## Patient Rights

Tokyo Police Hospital recognizes and upholds the following rights for all patients:

### 1. Right to Appropriate Medical Care
Every patient has the right to receive appropriate, safe, and high-quality medical care without discrimination based on gender, age, nationality, religion, disability, or any other characteristic.

### 2. Right to Information
Patients have the right to receive clear and understandable information about:
- Their diagnosis and medical condition
- Proposed treatments and procedures
- Expected outcomes, benefits, and risks
- Alternative treatment options
- The names and qualifications of the healthcare professionals involved in their care

### 3. Right to Informed Consent
Patients have the right to make informed decisions about their care. No procedure or treatment will be carried out without the patient's consent, except in medical emergencies where the patient cannot give consent.

### 4. Right to Refuse Treatment
Patients have the right to refuse any proposed treatment or procedure after being fully informed of the consequences. This decision will be respected and documented.

### 5. Right to Privacy and Confidentiality
All personal and medical information is protected under Japanese privacy law and our hospital's privacy policy. Information will not be shared with third parties without the patient's consent, except as required by law or for direct medical purposes.

### 6. Right to a Second Opinion
Patients have the right to seek a second medical opinion from another physician, inside or outside our hospital. We will cooperate fully in facilitating second opinions.

### 7. Right to Access Medical Records
Patients have the right to access their own medical records. Please see [Medical Records Disclosure]({{< relref "/support/medical-records" >}}) for the procedure.

### 8. Right to Complaint and Feedback
Patients have the right to voice concerns, complaints, or suggestions without fear of negative consequences to their care. Please speak with the nursing staff, a patient liaison, or submit feedback in writing.

## Patient Responsibilities

In return, we ask that patients and their families uphold the following responsibilities:

### 1. Provide Accurate Information
Please provide accurate and complete information about your medical history, current medications, allergies, and any other relevant health information. Withholding or misrepresenting information may compromise your safety and the quality of care you receive.

### 2. Follow Medical Advice
Please follow the treatment plan agreed upon with your physician and nursing team. If you do not understand or do not agree with any part of your treatment, please ask for clarification before making changes.

### 3. Respect Hospital Rules
Please follow hospital rules including visiting hours, smoking restrictions, mobile phone policies, and infection control guidelines. These rules are in place to protect all patients, visitors, and staff.

### 4. Respect Staff and Other Patients
Please treat all hospital staff and fellow patients with courtesy and respect. Abusive, threatening, or violent behavior toward staff or other patients will not be tolerated and may result in discharge or refusal of future services.

### 5. Financial Responsibility
Patients are responsible for the costs of their medical care in accordance with their health insurance coverage and any applicable co-payments or surcharges.

---

We believe that a strong partnership between patients and healthcare providers, built on mutual respect and trust, leads to the best possible outcomes for everyone.
