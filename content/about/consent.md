## Comprehensive consent regarding testing and medical treatment

At our hospital, regarding tests and medical procedures that place a large burden on patients, we provide explanations to patients individually in accordance with the hospital's internal arrangements, confirm consent, and create a consent form. However, for the following examinations and medical practices that are considered to have a light burden on patients, the following educational medical practices established based on certain standards as a teaching hospital, specific actions by nurses, and training for specific actions, we will provide explanations and confirm consent verbally or by posting notices (referred to as comprehensive consent).

### 1) General items

Interview, inspection, palpation, body temperature measurement, physical measurement, blood pressure measurement, rehabilitation, nutritional guidance, determination of meal contents, photography of affected areas with camera, etc.

### 2) Various inspections and monitoring

Blood tests, immunological tests, infectious disease tests (including HIV tests), urine and fecal tests, microbiological tests (including collection of sputum, saliva, etc.), pathological and cytological examinations of specimens, various physiological function tests (electrocardiogram, respiratory function, pulse wave, ultrasound, electroencephalogram, electromyogram, etc.), X-ray photography, X-rays Fluoroscopic imaging, CT and MRI examinations that do not use contrast agents, psychological tests, transcutaneous oxygen saturation measurement tests, arterial pressure measurement tests, intraoperative monitoring (BIS monitor, muscle relaxation monitor, etc.), various intradermal reaction tests (patch test, intradermal test, tuberculin skin reaction, etc.), etc.

### 3) Treatment/medication/injection

Venous blood sampling, arterial blood sampling, arterial indwelling needle insertion, suction of sputum, etc., indwelling of nasogastric tubes and bladder indwelling catheters, treatment of oral care, etc., wound treatment (suture removal, small skin incisions, sutures, insertion, fixation, and removal of tubes and drains, simple debridement, etc.), percutaneous biopsy, bougie insertion, intranasal treatment, oral treatment, eyelash removal, nail cutting, eye drop treatment, etc. Regular medications, injections, peripheral intravenous indwelling needle insertion, continuous subcutaneous indwelling needle insertion, oxygen administration, etc. *The above medical procedures are performed by people with a certain level of experience, but in rare cases bleeding, numbness, anaphylaxis (allergic reaction), or other unexpected complications may occur. Treatment for such complications is provided as normal insurance treatment.

### 4) Recording and saving images

When necessary for medical treatment, still images and videos taken of examinations and medical procedures in examination rooms, operating rooms, intensive care units, fluoroscopy rooms, angiography rooms, MRI rooms, CT rooms, etc. will be stored and managed on a dedicated server within the hospital that is separated from the outside world.

## Regarding off-label use of drugs, etc.

### Regarding off-label use of pharmaceuticals and use of unapproved drugs when necessary for treatment

Pharmaceuticals and medical devices are required to be used in a manner approved by the Ministry of Health, Labor and Welfare based on the Pharmaceutical and Medical Device Act, but due to the necessity of treatment, they may be used in a manner that does not necessarily match the approved content (off-label use) or unapproved drugs may be used. In this case, our hospital will consider whether there are any problems with the efficacy and safety of the drug and approve it before using it. When using off-label or unapproved drugs, doctors and others usually explain to the patient using explanatory documents and obtain their consent. However, in cases where there is sufficient scientific evidence and it is recognized that it will be beneficial to the patient, a hospital meeting approves that the explanation and consent obtained through documents can be exceptionally simplified. Side effects caused by off-label use or use of unapproved drugs are not covered by the government's "Relief System for Adverse Drug Reactions." Patients can confirm the treatment details and refuse treatment.

## Regarding the use of medical information

While our hospital is a medical facility, we also focus on education and research to provide better medical care. When you receive treatment or testing at our hospital, the details of the treatment, test results, images, etc. will be saved as medical information. In addition, test samples such as blood and urine used in tests, samples taken during biopsies for pathological diagnosis, and samples such as tissue removed during surgery are stored as necessary for medical treatment, but these medical information and samples may be used for medical research and educational purposes. When using medical information and samples, please be careful in handling them in accordance with our hospital regulations and various guidelines, and we ask for your understanding and cooperation as your name and identity will never be revealed.

## Regarding information registration for various registered businesses

Our hospital participates in various registration projects run by academic societies and other organizations for the purpose of improving the quality of medical care. When registering information for these various registration projects, we strive to protect personal information by using a method that does not allow individuals to be identified by the recipient, but patients can withdraw their information registration at any time at their will. Please note that even if you do not consent to the provision of information, there will be no disadvantage to your medical treatment, but you may not be able to cancel the information that has already been registered.

## Regarding the use of security cameras and surveillance cameras

At our hospital, in order to protect the rights and safety of patients and staff, and to manage risks such as preventing troubles and various accidents, we permanently install security cameras on the premises and in common areas of the hospital.We may also install surveillance cameras for patients in hospital rooms for medical reasons or other reasons. Information regarding the installation of security cameras will be posted on this website as well as on notice boards within the hospital. Regarding surveillance cameras, it is necessary to protect the privacy of hospitalized patients, so we ask for written consent each time. In addition, from the perspective of protecting personal information, the video footage taken will be deleted within two weeks under the supervision of the person responsible for handling it, based on the "Tokyo Police Hospital Security Camera and Surveillance Camera Operational Standards" and the "Tokyo Police Hospital Personal Information Protection Regulations," and regulations prohibit it from being used for purposes other than those legitimated by law, etc., so please understand these rules before visiting our hospital.

If you have any questions about the above comprehensive consent or wish to express your disagreement, please contact your doctor or the Patient Support Office. Please note that even if you do not consent, it will not affect your medical treatment and you will not be disadvantaged. *For inquiries regarding tests and treatment for specific patients, please contact the doctor in charge.
