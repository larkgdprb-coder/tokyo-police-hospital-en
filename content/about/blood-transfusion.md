## Policy on Patient Refusal of Blood Transfusion

Tokyo Police Hospital respects every patient's right to make informed decisions about their medical care, including the right to refuse specific treatments on religious, personal, or ethical grounds.

## Our Approach

When a patient requests that blood transfusion be withheld or refused, our hospital follows the guidelines established by the relevant Japanese medical societies and the Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare.

### Informed Discussion

If you or a family member holds religious or personal beliefs against blood transfusion (for example, as a Jehovah's Witness), please inform the medical team as early as possible — ideally before admission or surgical procedures.

Our physicians will:
- Discuss your concerns and beliefs with respect and empathy
- Explain the medical risks associated with refusing blood transfusion for your specific condition and procedure
- Explore available alternatives to blood transfusion (bloodless surgery techniques, autologous blood collection, erythropoiesis-stimulating agents, etc.)
- Document your decision and preferences in your medical record

### Absolute Refusal vs. Relative Refusal

Tokyo Police Hospital distinguishes between:

- **Absolute refusal**: The patient refuses blood transfusion under any circumstances, including life-threatening situations.
- **Relative refusal**: The patient prefers to avoid blood transfusion but consents to it in life-threatening emergencies.

The patient's position will be carefully documented and confirmed in writing before any planned procedure.

### For Patients Who Absolutely Refuse Blood Transfusion

If a patient absolutely refuses blood transfusion even in life-threatening situations, and this decision cannot be altered through further discussion:

- We will document the patient's decision and obtain written confirmation.
- We will attempt to perform the procedure using maximum bloodless techniques.
- If our hospital determines that we cannot safely perform the procedure under the stated conditions, we may need to refer the patient to an institution better equipped to accommodate this request.
- The decision of a competent adult patient will be honored.

### For Minors and Patients Unable to Consent

Special consideration applies when the patient is a minor or lacks decision-making capacity. In such cases, legal guardians and next of kin will be consulted. If a minor's life is at risk, the hospital may seek legal guidance or court authorization to administer necessary blood products.

## Our Commitment

We are committed to treating every patient with respect, regardless of their beliefs. Our goal is always to find the best possible medical solution that aligns with the patient's wishes and values while ensuring the highest standard of safety.

For questions about this policy, please speak with your attending physician or contact the Patient Services desk.
