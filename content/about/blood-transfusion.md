---

At our hospital, we perform medical treatment with sufficient explanation and consent based on the premise of providing standard medical care. Regarding blood transfusions, we strive to provide treatment without blood transfusions as much as possible, but if a doctor determines that a blood transfusion has the potential to save or sustain life, we have a policy of ``relatively no blood transfusions,'' which prioritizes human life. For this reason, we will not sign any documents or certificates of exemption agreeing to ``absolute no blood transfusions'' under any circumstances. In addition, in an emergency situation where it is determined that there is no way to save life without a blood transfusion, a blood transfusion will be performed even without consent.
