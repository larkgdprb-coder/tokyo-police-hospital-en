## Hospital Mission

> **"Pursuing excellence in medical quality and patient satisfaction"**

At Tokyo Police Hospital, we are committed to providing the highest standard of medical care while placing the well-being, dignity, and satisfaction of every patient at the center of everything we do.

## Five Basic Policies

We guide all our activities by the following five principles:

### 1. Patient-Centered Care
We respect the rights, autonomy, and individual needs of every patient. All medical decisions are made collaboratively, with patients fully informed and actively involved in their care.

### 2. Safety First
Patient safety is our highest priority. We continuously monitor, review, and improve our safety systems to prevent medical errors and adverse events. All staff are trained in safety management protocols.

### 3. Evidence-Based Medicine
Our clinical practices are grounded in the latest scientific evidence. We actively participate in medical research, clinical trials, and continuing professional education to ensure our care meets or exceeds the highest standards.

### 4. Teamwork and Collaboration
We believe that the best outcomes are achieved through effective teamwork. Our multidisciplinary teams — including physicians, nurses, pharmacists, dietitians, social workers, and therapists — collaborate closely to coordinate care for each patient.

### 5. Community Partnership
We are committed to serving not only police officers and their families, but also the wider community of Nakano and greater Tokyo. We work in partnership with local clinics, emergency services, and community organizations to ensure accessible, continuous care for all.

## Clinical Ethics Guidelines

All clinical activities at Tokyo Police Hospital are conducted in accordance with our Clinical Ethics Guidelines, which establish:

- Respect for patient autonomy and informed consent
- Confidentiality and protection of medical information
- Ethical standards in research and clinical trials
- Procedures for resolving clinical ethical dilemmas
- Support for end-of-life care decisions

## Infection Control Policy

We maintain a rigorous infection control program, including:

- Active surveillance of hospital-acquired infections
- Standard and transmission-based precautions
- Antimicrobial stewardship (AST)
- Regular staff education and training
- Environmental disinfection protocols

## Patient Safety Management

Our Patient Safety Management Policy includes:

- A hospital-wide safety management committee
- Mandatory reporting and analysis of incident reports
- Root cause analysis for serious adverse events
- Regular safety training and simulation exercises
- Public reporting of safety indicators

## Non-Smoking Policy

Tokyo Police Hospital is a **completely non-smoking facility**. Smoking is not permitted anywhere on hospital grounds, including buildings, car parks, and outdoor areas. This policy applies to patients, visitors, and staff alike.
