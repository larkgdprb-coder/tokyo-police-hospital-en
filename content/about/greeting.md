The Tokyo Police Hospital was opened in Fujimi, Chiyoda Ward in 1929 with joint funding from all employees of the Metropolitan Police Department, and was relocated to a new building in Nakano Ward in 2008. Due to this background, we fulfill our social mission as a workplace hospital that treats Metropolitan Police Department employees who have been injured or sick due to official accidents, and at the same time serve as a regional core hospital that is also available to general patients and provides medical treatment to many local residents. Our hospital's philosophy is ``Improving the quality of medical care and patient satisfaction.'' We aim to provide safe, secure, and high-quality medical care that satisfies our patients, respecting the rights and wishes of our patients, and using the latest medical equipment and advanced medical technology by our excellent staff in a lush environment.




Medical care in recent years has become more sophisticated and more complex. In this day and age, it is extremely important to explain medical care in an easy-to-understand manner and provide informed consent for patients to understand, in order to build a relationship of trust between medical professionals and patients. At our hospital, we are working hard to put this into practice. In addition, although there have been reports of medical accidents recently, it is most important for patients to receive safe and secure medical care.Our hospital considers the promotion of medical safety to be an important issue, and is actively working on establishing safety measures and educating staff. In the medical department, we have established 30 clinical departments and 10 integrated centers for each specialty, creating a system that allows us to treat a large number of patients with a variety of illnesses and provide sufficient medical care. In particular, our Stroke Center, Endoscopy Center, Artificial Joint Center, Breast Center, etc. have received high praise for providing many of the latest and most advanced treatments.




Emergency treatment, which is of interest to the local community, is centered around our emergency center, which treats more than 5,000 patients a year who are transported by ambulance, and also accepts an even larger number of general emergency patients. We will further enhance and strengthen our emergency system so that we can respond to emergency medical requests as much as possible and provide patients with peace of mind. In the future, we will continue to contribute to the safety of society through medical care as a workplace hospital, and will place emphasis on enriching local medical care, and as a ``hospital for the benefit of the local community,'' we will work together with all of our staff to practice high-quality medical care in order to further contribute to the health of everyone. We appreciate your continued support.



Tokyo Police Hospital Director Shunji Hasegawa
