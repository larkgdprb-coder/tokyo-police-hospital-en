## Clinical Trials & Medical Research

Tokyo Police Hospital is committed to advancing medical knowledge and improving patient care through rigorous clinical research. We participate in clinical trials and observational studies in collaboration with academic institutions, research organizations, and pharmaceutical companies.

## What Are Clinical Trials?

Clinical trials are research studies that test new treatments, medications, diagnostic procedures, or care approaches in human participants. They are conducted in accordance with strict ethical guidelines and regulatory requirements to ensure patient safety and scientific validity.

Clinical trials go through multiple phases:
- **Phase I** — Safety assessment in a small group
- **Phase II** — Effectiveness and safety in a larger group
- **Phase III** — Comparison with current standard treatment
- **Phase IV** — Post-marketing surveillance

## Ethical Standards

All clinical research at Tokyo Police Hospital is conducted in compliance with:
- The **Declaration of Helsinki** (international ethical standards for medical research)
- Japan's **Act on the Protection of Personal Information**
- Japan's **Clinical Trials Act** (*Rinshō Kenkyūhō*)
- Guidelines from the **Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare**
- Review and approval by our **Institutional Review Board (IRB)** / **Ethics Committee**

## Participation is Voluntary

Participation in any clinical trial or research study is **entirely voluntary**. You will never be enrolled in a study without your full, informed consent. Refusing to participate — or withdrawing from a study at any time — will **never affect the quality of care you receive**.

## Informed Consent for Research

Before enrolling in any study, you will receive:
- A detailed written explanation of the study purpose, procedures, risks, and benefits
- The opportunity to ask questions and receive clear answers
- Time to consider your decision without pressure
- A signed copy of the informed consent form

## Patient Benefits

Participating in a clinical trial may give you access to:
- New treatments not yet widely available
- Closer medical monitoring during the study period
- The opportunity to contribute to medical advances that may benefit others

## Current Trials

Information about specific clinical trials currently enrolling patients at our hospital is available in Japanese on our hospital's notice boards and website, or by contacting your treating physician.
