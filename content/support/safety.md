## Medical safety system

① We have assigned a dedicated risk manager. ②We collect information, analyze, formulate countermeasures, provide feedback, and evaluate information to prevent medical accidents, and work to reduce incidents. ③We carry out safety rounds and medical safety campaigns to foster a culture of safety. ④We prevent incidents involving new employees through various training sessions.

### About medical safety

Medical Safety Management Office Full-time Risk Manager Head Nurse Mitsuyo Yamazaki

We have more opportunities to learn about cutting-edge medical care and famous doctors through TV health programs, but how do you decide which hospital to visit? It depends on the type of medical treatment, but the first thing to consider is whether or not the hospital is reliable. Therefore, we would like to explain the "medical safety" that hospitals carry out so that everyone can receive safe and reliable medical care.

#### "Patients and families are partners in team medical care"

At hospitals, we strive to provide safe, high-quality medical care through so-called "team medical care," in which doctors, nurses, and other professionals work together and cooperate with each other to provide medical care. However, what is essential for "team medical care" is the cooperation of patients and their families, and it is most important to receive the necessary information from medical professionals, discuss it, and decide on the treatment that is best for them with their consent. Discussing "diagnosis and problems" and "treatment and risks (effects of treatment, risks of not treating)" with your healthcare provider, and understanding and consenting to the tests and treatments you will undergo will lead to medical safety.

#### "Preventing patient mistakes - Please tell us your name and date of birth."

In 1999, there was a medical accident in which different surgeries were performed on different patients. The basis of medical safety is "identification". When receiving testing or treatment at our hospital, we will ask the patient to provide their name and date of birth to confirm their identity. Furthermore, while in the hospital, we ensure safety by authenticating and confirming patients with an ID band worn on their wrist. We apologize for the inconvenience of having to tell you your name and date of birth over and over again, but in order to provide safer medical care, we ask for your cooperation in telling us your name and date of birth.
