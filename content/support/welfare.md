## Medical Welfare Consultation Office (Medical Social Work)

Our **Medical Social Workers (MSWs)** are available to provide free, confidential support to patients and their families on a wide range of non-medical concerns that arise during illness and hospitalization.

## Hours & Contact

| | |
|---|---|
| **Hours** | Monday – Saturday, 8:30 – 17:00 |
| **Location** | 1st Floor (ask at the Information Desk or nursing station) |
| **Phone** | 03-5343-5611 (main) → ask for Medical Welfare Consultation Office |

**All consultations are free of charge and strictly confidential.**

## Who Should Use This Service?

This service is for any patient or family member who has questions or concerns related to:

- **Medical costs and financial assistance**
  - Difficulty paying hospital bills
  - Information about public assistance programs (High-Cost Medical Expense benefit, disability subsidies, welfare benefits)
  - Insurance-related questions

- **Discharge planning**
  - Concerns about returning home after hospitalization
  - Need for home nursing or home care support
  - Transition to rehabilitation hospitals or long-term care facilities

- **Home care and community services**
  - Setting up home care (home nurse visits, home helpers, day care services)
  - Nursing care insurance (*Kaigo Hoken*) applications and assessments
  - Linking to community-based support services

- **Welfare services**
  - Information about social welfare programs
  - Applications for disability certificates (*Shōgai-sha Techō*)
  - Support for elderly patients and their families

- **Psychological and social concerns**
  - Anxiety about illness and treatment
  - Family burden and caregiver support
  - End-of-life planning and advance care planning

## How to Access This Service

You do not need a physician's referral to consult with a medical social worker. You can:
1. Ask any nurse or reception staff to connect you with the MSW office
2. Visit the Medical Welfare Consultation Office on the 1st Floor directly
3. Call the main hospital number and ask to be connected

Family members may also consult with the MSW on behalf of a patient (with patient consent).

## About Our Social Workers

Our social workers are licensed **Medical Social Workers** (*Shakai Fukushi-shi* or *Seishin Hoken Fukushi-shi*) with experience in hospital-based social work. They work closely with the clinical team to support the whole person — not just the medical condition — and to ensure that discharge is safe, well-planned, and supported.
