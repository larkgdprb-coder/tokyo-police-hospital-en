## Medical Records Disclosure

In accordance with Japanese law and our hospital's patient rights policy, patients have the right to access and obtain copies of their own medical records. We handle all requests in compliance with the Act on the Protection of Personal Information.

## Application Procedure

1. Submit a completed **Medical Records Disclosure Application Form** (available at the Document Counter ⑦, 1F, or on the hospital's Japanese website).
2. Present valid photo ID (passport, driver's license, or My Number card with photo).
3. If applying on behalf of a patient:
   - Written authorization from the patient
   - Applicant's own photo ID
   - Relationship documentation if applicable (family register, etc.)
4. Pay the applicable fees.
5. Records will be prepared and made available within **approximately 2–4 weeks** of application.

## What Can Be Disclosed

The following types of records may be requested:
- Outpatient consultation records
- Inpatient records and nursing notes
- Operative records
- Laboratory and test results
- Radiology and imaging reports
- Discharge summaries

## What May Be Withheld

In limited circumstances, certain information may be withheld if disclosure could:
- Cause significant harm to the patient's health or well-being
- Infringe on the privacy of third parties (e.g., other patients mentioned in records)

In such cases, the hospital will provide a written explanation of the reason for non-disclosure.

## Posthumous Disclosure

Medical records of deceased patients may be disclosed to family members (next of kin) under specific conditions. Please contact the Document Counter for guidance.
