## Multidisciplinary Support Teams

Tokyo Police Hospital operates a comprehensive network of **multidisciplinary support teams** that work across all departments to ensure the highest standards of patient safety, care quality, and clinical outcomes.

Each team brings together specialists from different disciplines — physicians, nurses, pharmacists, dietitians, therapists, and others — to provide expert support to clinical teams and patients throughout the hospital.

---

### ICT — Infection Control Team

The **Infection Control Team (ICT)** monitors, prevents, and manages hospital-acquired infections throughout the facility.

**Activities include:**
- Surveillance of infection rates (SSI, VAP, CLABSI, CAUTI, etc.)
- Implementation of standard and transmission-based precautions
- Investigation and management of outbreak situations
- Education and training for all hospital staff
- Environmental infection control (disinfection, cleaning protocols)

---

### AST — Antimicrobial Stewardship Team

The **Antimicrobial Stewardship Team (AST)** promotes the appropriate use of antibiotics and antifungal medications to improve patient outcomes and reduce the emergence of drug-resistant organisms.

**Activities include:**
- Review of antibiotic prescriptions for appropriateness
- De-escalation guidance when culture results are available
- Education on antimicrobial resistance
- Monitoring and reporting of resistance patterns

---

### Palliative Care Team

The **Palliative Care Team** provides holistic support for patients with life-limiting illnesses and their families, focusing on quality of life, symptom management, and emotional well-being.

**Services include:**
- Pain management consultation
- Symptom control (nausea, breathlessness, fatigue, etc.)
- Psychological and spiritual support
- Family communication and bereavement support
- Advance care planning guidance

---

### NST — Nutrition Support Team

The **Nutrition Support Team (NST)** assesses and manages the nutritional needs of patients at risk of malnutrition, particularly in complex inpatient cases.

**Activities include:**
- Nutritional screening for all inpatients
- Development of individualized nutrition care plans
- Management of enteral (tube feeding) and parenteral (IV) nutrition
- Monitoring and adjustment of nutritional therapy

---

### RST — Respiratory Support Team

The **Respiratory Support Team (RST)** provides specialist support for patients requiring respiratory therapy, including mechanical ventilation and non-invasive ventilation.

**Activities include:**
- Assessment of patients with respiratory failure
- Optimization of ventilator settings
- Weaning from mechanical ventilation
- Coordination of respiratory rehabilitation

---

### Urinary Care Team

The **Urinary Care Team** focuses on the prevention and management of urinary tract infections (UTIs) associated with urinary catheters, and the promotion of bladder function.

**Activities include:**
- Catheter-associated UTI prevention protocols
- Early removal of unnecessary catheters
- Bladder training and rehabilitation

---

### Early Mobilization & Rehabilitation Team

This team promotes early, safe mobilization of patients to prevent the complications of prolonged bed rest, including muscle weakness, deep vein thrombosis, and delirium.

**Activities include:**
- Assessment of mobility and rehabilitation needs
- Coordination of physiotherapy, occupational therapy, and nursing care
- Goal-setting for early recovery and discharge

---

### Pressure Ulcer Prevention Team

This team works to prevent and manage pressure ulcers (bedsores) in at-risk patients throughout the hospital.

**Activities include:**
- Pressure ulcer risk screening (Braden Scale)
- Implementation of prevention protocols (repositioning, special mattresses)
- Wound care and management consultation

---

### Diabetes Dialysis Prevention Team

This team supports patients with diabetes to prevent progression to dialysis by optimizing blood glucose management and renal protection.

**Activities include:**
- Diabetic nephropathy education
- Collaboration with nephrology and endocrinology teams
- Lifestyle guidance and self-management support

---

### RRS — Rapid Response System

The **Rapid Response System (RRS)** is designed to identify and respond to patients showing early signs of clinical deterioration before a serious event occurs.

**Activities include:**
- Continuous monitoring of early warning signs
- Rapid Response Team (RRT) available 24/7 to respond to bedside calls from nurses
- Post-event review and system improvement

---

### Dementia & Delirium Care Support Team

This team provides specialist assessment and management of patients with dementia and/or delirium during hospitalization.

**Activities include:**
- Cognitive assessment on admission
- Prevention and management of hospital-acquired delirium
- Staff education on dementia-friendly care
- Family support and communication

---

### Physical Restraint Optimization Team

This team works to minimize the use of physical restraints in clinical settings, promoting alternative strategies that protect patient dignity while ensuring safety.

**Activities include:**
- Review of restraint use across the hospital
- Education on restraint-free alternatives
- Policy development and audit

---

## Requesting a Team Consultation

Any member of the clinical team can request a consultation from any support team. Patients and families who believe they may benefit from a specific team's expertise are welcome to mention this to their nurse or physician.
